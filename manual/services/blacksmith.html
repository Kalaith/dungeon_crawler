Blacksmith

Overview

From battle to battle, warriors' swords suffer wear and tear. Their edges grow dull and eventually, they may break. Armor, too, can be broken from a strong blow. Needless to say, a breastplate hanging in pieces from a character's shoulders doesn't do much to protect them. If your characters' goods are in such a shape, get them to a smith to make the repairs.

Blacksmith Services

Talk

Having seen the battle-worn pass through their doors, smiths can offer the party a tale or two about combat around the area or an inside tip on the best way to use a weapon. The weapons traders who frequent their establishment also leave behind their news, and the smith might pass the information along to you... if they take a liking to you that is.

Have Something Repaired

That fine two-handed sword you're carrying - the hilt in one hand, the blade in the other - isn't going to do you or your party much good in combat. To have your sword or armor fixed, select Have Something Repaired.

Repair Process:

  1. Select Have Something Repaired
  2. You will see a list of the character's items and the cost to repair each
  3. Select one, and the smith will quote their fee
  4. Choose to accept, haggle, or decline

Haggle

Think the smith is asking too much to put your armor back together? If you do, select haggle, a character to do it, and finally the percentage reduction you're looking for. If the merchant agrees to your offer, they will begin work. Of course, you may enter 0 if you feel they're giving you a good deal to begin with.

Important: Keep in mind that metalwork, like any trade, is not exacting nor does it happen at the speed of light. You may have to leave your weapon, and there is a chance that upon returning you'll find your sword or armor is beyond repair or at least beyond the skills of the smith you're working with.

Next Page and Previous Page

If your character is carrying lots of items, Next Page will move you further into their inventory. Previous Page returns you to the previous page.

Another Character

Sometimes your journey has been so long, your battles so hard, that numerous characters come back with their armor and swords in pieces. Select the Another Character option to bring your characters before the smith one by one.

Exit

Select Exit to return to the Main Smith options.

Leave The Smith

When you've finished tending to your swords' wounds, select Leave The Smith to return to the streets of town.

Repair Strategy

When To Repair

  • Regular Inspections: Check equipment condition regularly
  • After Major Battles: Repair damage before it worsens
  • Before Expeditions: Ensure all equipment is in good condition before wilderness travel
  • Broken Items: Repair immediately - broken equipment is useless

Cost Management

  • Use haggle skill to reduce repair costs
  • Prioritize repairs for essential equipment first
  • Compare prices between different smiths if available
  • Consider if replacement might be cheaper than repair for low-quality items

Equipment Maintenance

Priority Item Type Reason
High Primary Weapons Essential for combat effectiveness
High Heavy Armor Critical for front-line fighters' survival
Medium Secondary Weapons Backup equipment still important
Medium Shields Provides defensive bonuses
Low Light Armor Less critical but still useful

Repair Risks

Potential Issues:

  • Time Required: Repairs take time; you may need to leave equipment
  • Skill Variation: Not all smiths have equal skill
  • Beyond Repair: Some items may be too damaged to fix
  • Failed Repairs: Smith may fail to repair item

Alternative Options

  • Replacement: Buy new equipment instead of repairing old
  • Backup Equipment: Carry spare weapons to swap when primary breaks
  • Different Smith: Try another smith if first one fails
  • Delay: If not urgent, wait until reaching a town with more skilled smiths

Information Gathering

Don't forget to Talk to smiths:

  • Learn about local combat situations
  • Get tips on weapon usage
  • Hear news from weapons traders
  • Discover information about nearby dangers
