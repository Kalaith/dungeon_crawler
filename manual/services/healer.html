Healer

Overview

Your magician's astral points are wiped out and your most skilled healer is still trying to shake arrows out of their arm. It's a tough situation to be in. If a character lets a wound go too long, they will likely become infected or diseased. Thankfully, towns throughout the world offer the services of healers who can help your party heal its wounds.

Healer Services

Treat Wounds

After a vicious battle, your proud but worn characters may have a few wounds that need tending. Selecting Treat Wounds and a character in need, the healer surveys their damage, notes the price, and allows you to accept or reject the proposal. Should you hand over your crowns, the healer begins to work their trade.

  • Healer quotes a price based on injury severity
  • You can accept or reject the offer
  • Hopefully, if the healer is skilled, they will heal your character
  • Warning: Certain healers have no medical talent but use the healer banner merely to make money

Treat Illness

Characters may incur numerous maladies. Luckily, skilled healers can offer the proper cure for most any illness. Should one of your characters require such an elixir, select Treat Illness followed by the ill character.

Treatment Process:

  1. Select Treat Illness
  2. Choose the ill character
  3. Healer reviews symptoms and work necessary to cure it
  4. Healer quotes a price which you may take or leave
  5. If you accept, the mystic begins their handiwork
  6. If successful, the fee is collected in full
  7. If cure is not successful, the healer will only charge a portion of quoted price

Neutralize Poison

Along with wounds and illnesses, many healers can treat poison and neutralize its devastating effects. Just select this option and the afflicted character, and the healer will suggest a price. If you accept, they begin their work.

Payment Terms: As with illnesses, if the healer is not successful, they will only ask for a portion of the total amount due.

Leave Healer

When your characters have been nursed and their ills no longer affect them, select this option to return to the out of doors.

Service Comparison

Service Treats Payment
Treat Wounds Physical damage, injuries from combat Full price regardless of outcome
Treat Illness Diseases and maladies Full price if successful, partial if not
Neutralize Poison Poisoned status and poison effects Full price if successful, partial if not

Strategy Tips

When To Visit Healers

  • After Major Battles: Treat wounds before they become infected
  • Disease Symptoms: Visit immediately upon noticing illness
  • Poisoning: Seek treatment as soon as possible to prevent damage
  • Low LP: When party's healing resources are exhausted
  • Party Healer Down: When your own healer is injured or ill

Cost Management

  • Compare prices between different healers if multiple are available
  • Consider using party's own healing skills first to save money
  • Seek healers for serious conditions your party can't handle
  • Remember: partial refund if treatment fails (except wounds)

Healer Quality

Important: Not all healers are equally skilled. Some are charlatans with no real medical talent. If a healer repeatedly fails treatments, consider finding another healer in a different town.

Alternative Healing

Before visiting a healer, consider these alternatives:

  • Party Skills: Use Treat Wounds, Treat Disease, or Treat Poison skills
  • Magic: Healing spells can cure wounds and ailments
  • Potions and Herbs: Use healing items from inventory
  • Inn Rest: Sleep at an inn for natural healing
  • Temple Miracles: Ask deities for divine healing

Prevention

  • Keep party well-equipped with armor to prevent wounds
  • Maintain stock of healing potions for emergencies
  • Treat minor wounds immediately to prevent infection
  • Avoid unnecessary combat when party is weakened
  • Use caution in areas known for disease or poison
