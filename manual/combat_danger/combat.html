Combat System

Overview

Combat is turn-based and tactical, where every battle matters. Random encounters trigger fully realized turn-based battles, not quick auto-resolve events. Even regular enemies can severely damage or kill party members. Strategic skill usage, proper formation, and careful resource management are essential for survival.

Core Philosophy: Every battle is meaningful. Regular enemies are deadly, FOEs can instantly kill unprepared parties, and boss fights require dozens of turns of careful planning. Status effects, buffs, and debuffs are genuinely important.

Combat Structure

The Combat Round

Combat is organized into **Rounds**. A round represents about 6 seconds in the game world. During a round, every participant takes a **Turn**.

  1. Initiative: At the start of combat, everyone rolls Initiative (d20 + AG Modifier) to determine the Turn Order. Speed determines who acts first.
  2. Turns: Participants act in order from highest Initiative to lowest.
  3. New Round: Once everyone has acted, the round ends and a new one begins (keeping the same Initiative order).

Action Economy: Your Turn

On your turn, you can move and take actions. You generally have the following resources available:

1. Action (Main)

The main thing you do on your turn.

  • Attack: Make a melee or ranged attack. (Some classes/monsters get Multiattack, allowing multiple hits).
  • Cast Spell: Cast a spell with a casting time of "1 Action".
  • Defend (Dodge): Focus on defense (Disadvantage to be hit).
  • Use Item: Drink a potion or use a tool.
  • Skill: Use a special class ability.
  • Dash: Double your movement speed (irrelevant in static rows, but useful for escaping).
  • Disengage: Prevent opportunity attacks when moving.

2. Bonus Action (Quick)

A quick action available only if a specific ability, spell, or weapon grants it.

  • Off-hand Attack: If dual-wielding light weapons.
  • Quick Spells: Spells like Healing Word or Misty Step.
  • Class Features: Rogue's Cunning Action, Bardic Inspiration, etc.

3. Movement

Combat movement is restricted to switching rows. There are no terrain modifiers or grid movement in combat.

  • Switch Row: Moving between Front and Back rows costs movement.
  • Interact: You can interact with one object for free (draw weapon, open door).

4. Reaction (Instant)

A special action taken outside your turn in response to a trigger.

  • Opportunity Attack: Attack an enemy leaving your reach.
  • Spells: Counterspell, Shield, Hellish Rebuke.
  • Readied Action: An action you prepared to take when a specific condition is met.

Initiative & Speed

Turn order is decided by the Initiative Roll, which is based on your Agility (AG) and Intuition (IN).

Initiative = d20 + AG Modifier + IN Modifier (if IN ≥ 12)

  • Agility (AG): Primary initiative attribute (reaction speed)
  • Intuition (IN): Adds to initiative if 12+ (danger sense, allows you to act faster)
  • Ties: If tied, the character with higher AG goes first. If still tied, higher IN. If still tied, simultaneous actions.
  • Surprise: If a side is surprised, they cannot move or act on their first turn and can't take reactions until that turn ends.

Initiative Examples

Character 1: AG 10 (+0), IN 8 (+0) → Initiative = d20 + 0

Character 2: AG 12 (+1), IN 13 (+1) → Initiative = d20 + 2

Character 3: AG 13 (+1), IN 10 (+0) → Initiative = d20 + 1 (IN below 12, no bonus)

Party & Monster Size Limits

The combat system supports up to 6 characters in the player's party and up to 6 monsters in an encounter. Formation tables reflect these limits.

Row Positioning

Party members are arranged in two rows with significant tactical implications:

Row Damage Taken Melee Damage Dealt Ranged/Magic Best For
Front 100% (Normal) 100% (Normal) 100% (Normal) Melee Fighters & Battle Mages: Anyone with good AC or melee attacks. Mages using touch spells (e.g., Shocking Grasp) or cone spells (e.g., Burning Hands) often benefit from being here.
Back Melee attacks vs you have Disadvantage Disadvantage on Attack Rolls 100% (Normal) Ranged & Support: Archers, Healers, and fragile Casters. Protected from direct melee threats.

Formation Strategy

Recommended Formation

Front Row: 2-3 characters with high HP, armor, and melee capability (Warriors, Paladins, Barbarians)

Back Row: 2-3 characters with ranged attacks, magic, or healing (Magicians, Clerics, Rangers, Bards)

Row Switching

Characters can switch rows during combat:

  • Action Cost: Free action (no turn cost)
  • Tactical Use: Move wounded characters to back row for protection
  • Emergency Response: Bring melee fighters forward if back row is threatened
  • Skill Synergy: Some skills require specific row positioning

Combat Actions

Attack

Make a weapon attack against an enemy. Attacks use a two-step process: Attack Roll, then Damage Roll.

Attack Roll

Attack Roll = d20 + Attribute Modifier + Proficiency Bonus + Weapon Skill

If your attack roll meets or exceeds the target's Armor Class (AC), the attack hits and you roll damage.

Weapon Type Attribute Used Example
Melee Weapons Strength (ST) Longsword, Greatsword, Mace
Finesse Weapons ST or Dexterity (DX) - your choice Rapier, Shortsword, Dagger
Ranged Weapons (Bows) Agility (AG) Longbow, Shortbow
Ranged Weapons (Thrown) Dexterity (DX) Throwing Dagger, Javelin
Crossbows Dexterity (DX) Light Crossbow, Heavy Crossbow

Damage Calculation

Damage = Weapon Dice + Attribute Modifier

Weapon Damage Dice Modifier Example Damage
Dagger 1d4 + ST or DX 1d4 + 1 (if ST/DX +1)
Shortsword 1d6 + ST or DX 1d6 + 2 (if ST/DX +2)
Longsword 1d8 + ST 1d8 + 3 (if ST +3)
Greatsword 2d6 + ST 2d6 + 3 (if ST +3)
Longbow 1d8 + AG 1d8 + 2 (if AG +2)

Critical Hits

  • Trigger: Natural 20 on attack roll (automatic hit)
  • Damage: Roll weapon damage dice twice, then add modifiers once
  • Example: Longsword (1d8) crit with ST +2 = 2d8 + 2
  • Critical Miss: Natural 1 on attack roll (automatic miss)

Special Weapon Properties

  • Two-Handed: Add +2 to damage (Greatsword, Greataxe, Maul)
  • Versatile: Can be used one-handed or two-handed (Longsword: 1d8 one-handed, 1d10 two-handed)
  • Light: Can dual-wield (Dagger, Shortsword)
  • Finesse: Use ST or DX for attack and damage (your choice)

Row Penalties

  • Melee from Back Row: Attacks have Disadvantage (roll 2d20, take lower)
  • Ranged/Magic: No penalty from back row

Defend

Focus entirely on avoiding attacks during the turn.

  • Effect: Attackers have Disadvantage on attack rolls against you. You have Advantage on Agility saving throws.
  • Duration: Until the start of your next turn.
  • Use Case: Survive powerful enemy attacks, protect low HP characters.

Skill

Apply learned abilities - offensive, defensive, or support.

Offensive Skills

  • Single-target high damage attacks
  • Multi-target AOE attacks
  • Elemental attacks exploiting weaknesses
  • Status-inflicting attacks (poison, paralysis, sleep)

Defensive Skills

  • Taunt abilities forcing enemies to target tank
  • AC boosts (Shield, Parry)
  • Counter-attack stances
  • Evasion boosts (Dodge)

Support Skills

  • Attack/AC buffs (Bless, Shield of Faith)
  • Elemental resistance buffs
  • Speed manipulation
  • Debuffs reducing enemy AC or Attack Rolls

Item

Use consumable items from inventory on selected targets.

  • Healing Potions: Restore HP
  • Mana Potions: Restore TP/AP
  • Antidotes: Cure poison
  • Panaceas: Cure all status effects
  • Buff Items: Temporary stat boosts

Switch Row

Move character between front and back rows (free action, no turn cost).

Escape

Attempt to flee combat.

  • Success Rate: Based on party AG vs enemy AG
  • FOE Escape: Much harder due to FOE's high stats
  • Consequence: No experience or loot if successful
  • Failure: Enemies get free attacks

Magic (Spell Casting)

Cast a spell from your learned spell list.

Requirements

  • Sufficient Astral Points (AP)
  • Appropriate action type (action, bonus action, or reaction)
  • Valid target within range
  • Line of sight (for most spells)

Spell Types

Offensive Spells
  • Direct damage (Magic Missile, Fireball, Lightning Bolt)
  • Damage over time (Acid Arrow, Heat Metal)
  • Area of effect attacks
  • Status-inflicting spells (Hold Person, Sleep)
Defensive Spells
  • Protective barriers (Shield, Mage Armor)
  • Damage resistance (Protection from Energy)
  • Counterspells (Counterspell, Dispel Magic)
Support Spells
  • Healing (Cure Wounds, Healing Word, Mass Cure Wounds)
  • Buffs (Bless, Haste, Heroism)
  • Debuffs (Bane, Slow, Bestow Curse)
  • Utility (Invisibility, Fly, Misty Step)

Concentration Management

Only one concentration spell can be active at a time. Key considerations:

  • Taking damage risks breaking concentration (CON save required)
  • Strategic choice: maintain powerful buff or cast new spell?
  • Breaking enemy concentration is a valuable tactic
  • Position concentration casters in back row for protection

AP Management

  • Higher level spells cost more AP (see Magic & Spells)
  • Cantrips are free (0 AP) but less powerful
  • Balance powerful spells vs. resource conservation
  • Consider meditation to restore AP between combats

Status Effects

Ailments (Negative)

Ailment Effect Duration Cure
Poison Disadvantage on Attack Rolls and Ability Checks Until cured or battle ends Antidote, Cure Poison spell
Paralysis Incapacitated, Auto-fail ST/AG saves, Attacks vs you have Advantage and Auto-Crit if within 5ft Save ends Panacea, Cleanse spell
Sleep Unconscious, wake if damaged or shaken Until damaged Physical interaction, Wake spell

Buffs (Positive)

Buff Effect Duration Stacking
Bless +1d4 to Attack Rolls and Saving Throws 1 minute (Conc) No
Shield of Faith +2 AC 10 minutes (Conc) No
Haste Double Speed, +2 AC, Advantage on AG saves, Extra Action 1 minute (Conc) No

Debuffs (Enemy Weakening)

Debuffs are crucial for difficult encounters:

  • Bane: -1d4 to Enemy Attack Rolls and Saves
  • Slow: Half speed, -2 AC, no reactions, limited actions
  • Faerie Fire: Attacks vs Enemy have Advantage, no invisibility
  • Blindness: Enemy has Disadvantage on attacks

Critical Insight: Unlike most RPGs, buffs and debuffs are ESSENTIAL, not optional. A properly buffed/debuffed party can survive encounters that would otherwise be impossible.

Attack & Damage Mechanics

The Attack Roll

To determine if an attack hits, the attacker rolls a d20 and adds their attack bonus.

Attack Roll: d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus + Other Modifiers

If the total equals or exceeds the target's Armor Class (AC), the attack hits.

  • Natural 1: Automatic Miss.
  • Natural 20: Critical Hit (Automatic Hit + Double Damage Dice).

Damage Calculation

If an attack hits, damage is rolled based on the weapon or spell used.

Physical Damage: Weapon Dice + ST/DX Modifier

Spell Damage: Spell Dice + (sometimes) Spellcasting Modifier

Modifiers Include:

  • Critical Hit: Roll damage dice twice, then add modifiers.
  • Resistance: Target takes half damage.
  • Vulnerability: Target takes double damage.
  • Row Penalty: Melee attacks from back row have Disadvantage on the attack roll (roll 2d20, take lowest).

Combat Difficulty

Regular Encounters

Standard enemies are dangerous:

  • Can deal 20-40% of character's HP per hit
  • Often appear in groups of 3-5
  • May have elemental attacks or status effects
  • Require strategic skill usage, not just basic attacks

FOE Encounters

Mini-boss enemies with extreme stats:

  • 3-5x HP of regular enemies
  • Can one-shot unprepared characters
  • Often have multiple actions per turn
  • Require full party coordination and buff stacking
  • Yield superior materials and experience

Boss Encounters

Major battles requiring extensive preparation:

  • Battles last 20-40+ turns
  • Multiple phases with changing attack patterns
  • Require specific strategies and party compositions
  • Full resource management (HP/TP/items)
  • Often have adds (additional enemies) that spawn mid-fight

Combat Strategy

Pre-Combat Preparation

  • Ensure party is at full HP/TP before major encounters
  • Equip appropriate elemental resistance gear
  • Stock healing items and status cure items
  • Position fragile characters in back row
  • Identify enemy weaknesses (if known)

Opening Turns

  • Turn 1-2: Apply buffs (ATK/DEF up) and debuffs (enemy ATK/DEF down)
  • Turn 2-3: Apply elemental resistances if enemy uses elemental attacks
  • Turn 3+: Begin damage phase with buffed party

Mid-Combat Tactics

  • Maintain buff/debuff stacks - reapply before they expire
  • Focus fire priority targets (healers, casters)
  • Switch wounded characters to back row
  • Cure dangerous status effects immediately (paralysis, sleep)
  • Manage TP/AP carefully - don't waste on overkill
  • Track spell durations: Know when buffs/debuffs expire (typically 10 turns for 1-minute spells)
  • Protect concentration casters: Keep them in back row and use defensive positioning
  • Target enemy concentration: Break powerful enemy buffs by damaging their casters
  • Concentration decisions: Decide if maintaining current spell is better than casting a new one

Emergency Responses

  • If character drops below 30% HP, heal immediately
  • If multiple characters are low, use AOE healing
  • If party is overwhelmed, consider fleeing
  • Use defensive skills (Guard, DEF buffs) to survive burst damage
  • Revive fallen allies only if safe to do so

After Combat

Victory Rewards

  • Experience Points: Distributed to all surviving party members
  • Materials: Monster drops for crafting
  • Gold: Currency for purchases
  • Equipment: Rare drops from powerful enemies
  • First Encounter Bonus: Extra XP for first time facing enemy type

Post-Combat Actions

  • Treat wounds and poison immediately
  • Restore HP/TP if resources allow
  • Check inventory space before continuing
  • Assess party condition - return to town if weakened
  • Save at next save point

Combat Tips

  • Never underestimate regular enemies - they can kill you
  • Buffs and debuffs are not optional - use them
  • Status effects are genuinely dangerous - cure them immediately
  • Formation matters - protect your back row
  • Speed tuning allows healers to save dying allies
  • Elemental weaknesses can double your damage - exploit them
  • Don't waste TP/AP on trivial encounters - conserve for bosses
  • Fleeing is valid - survival is more important than pride
  • Learn enemy patterns - predictable attacks can be countered
  • Party synergy is crucial - build complementary teams
  • Concentration is limited: Choose your one concentration spell wisely
  • Cantrips are free: Use them liberally to save AP for critical moments
  • Pre-buff before bosses: Cast long-duration spells before entering combat
  • Ritual spells are free: Use them outside combat for utility (0 AP cost)
  • Break enemy concentration: Damaging enemy casters can end powerful buffs
  • Spell components simplified: Don't worry about components unless expensive materials are required
