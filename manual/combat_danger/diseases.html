Diseases

Overview

Characters may incur numerous maladies during their adventures. If a character lets a wound go too long untreated, they will likely become infected or diseased.

Contracting Diseases

  • Untreated Wounds: Wounds left untreated often lead to infection and disease
  • Environmental Hazards: Exposure to certain environments or creatures
  • Combat: Some enemies can inflict disease through special attacks
  • Poor Conditions: Lack of food, water, or rest can weaken constitution

Checking Disease Status

While reviewing your character, if they are ill, the "Sickness" option will appear on the menu. After selecting it, you will receive information about the character's condition.

Treatment Options

Treat Disease Skill

A character with the Treat Disease skill (CR/WD/CH) can attempt to cure illnesses. Several team members should be proficient with this healing skill.

  • Use during camp or character review
  • Select character with skill
  • Choose patient to treat
  • Success depends on skill level

Healer Services

Towns throughout the world offer the services of healers who can help cure diseases.

ServiceProcess
Treat Illness Healer reviews symptoms and quotes price. If successful, fee collected in full. If cure not successful, healer only charges portion of quoted price.

Magic Healing

Certain healing spells can cure diseases. Magic users with appropriate spells can cast them during camp or at character review.

Remedies and Potions

Special herbal remedies and magical potions can cure specific diseases. These can be brewed through Alchemy or purchased from merchants.

Disease Effects

Diseases can have various negative effects:

  • Reduced attribute values
  • Decreased combat effectiveness
  • Lower movement points
  • Gradual life point loss
  • Reduced skill performance
  • Inability to heal properly

Prevention

Immediate Wound Treatment

The best prevention is treating wounds immediately after combat. Use Treat Wounds skill or healing magic before wounds can become infected.

Maintain Health

  • Keep characters well-fed and hydrated
  • Ensure adequate rest at inns or camps
  • Avoid unnecessary exposure to hazards
  • Maintain good equipment condition

Early Detection

  • Regularly review character status
  • Check for Sickness option in menu
  • Monitor attribute values for unexplained drops
  • Treat symptoms early before they worsen

Strategy

Party Preparation

  • Have multiple party members with Treat Disease skill
  • Stock disease-curing potions and remedies
  • Keep magic users with healing spells
  • Locate healers in towns before exploring dangerous areas

During Adventure

  • Treat all wounds immediately after combat
  • Monitor character health regularly
  • Don't ignore minor illnesses - they can worsen
  • Return to town for professional treatment if needed

Resource Management

  • Always carry healing supplies
  • Save money for healer services
  • Don't exhaust all healing resources in one encounter
  • Rest at inns for natural recovery
