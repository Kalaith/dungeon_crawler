Magic & Spells

Overview

A well-honed blade is not the only way to face the powers of a fantasy world. A well-crafted magic spell can work miracles as well. Your team can use the arcane powers of the cosmos for their benefit or another's demise.

New Magic System (D&D 5e)

With the introduction of new classes like Cleric and Paladin, we have expanded our magic system to include the full 5th Edition spell list and schools of magic.

  • Schools of Magic - Learn about the 8 schools of magic.
  • Complete Spell List - Browse and filter all available spells by class, level, and school.

Arcane Lore

If you created a magician or Magician, you will be asked to choose the character's preferred arcane lore. Arcane lore influences the character's magical focus and affects spell casting skill for the selected category.

Astral Points (AP) System

All magic is powered by astral energy measured in Astral Points (AP). This resource pool replaces D&D's spell slot system, providing more flexibility while maintaining balance.

AP Pool Calculation

Base AP = 10 + (Intelligence Modifier × 2) + Class Bonus

Class AP Bonus (Level 1) Spellcasting Attribute
Wizard/Magician +20 AP Intelligence (IT)
Cleric +15 AP Wisdom (WD)
Druid +15 AP Wisdom (WD)
Warlock +12 AP Intuition (IN)
Sorcerer +12 AP Charisma (CH)
Bard +10 AP Charisma (CH)
Paladin +8 AP Charisma (CH)
Ranger +5 AP Wisdom (WD)

Example: Level 1 Wizard

Intelligence: 13 (Modifier +1)

Calculation: 10 + (1 × 2) + 20 = 32 AP

Result: Can cast six Level 1 spells (5 AP each) or two Level 3 spells (12 AP each)

Spell AP Costs (Balanced)

Spell costs are carefully balanced to prevent unlimited casting while maintaining flexibility:

Spell Level AP Cost Example Spells
Cantrip 0 Fire Bolt, Mage Hand, Sacred Flame
1st Level 5 Magic Missile, Cure Wounds, Shield
2nd Level 8 Scorching Ray, Hold Person, Invisibility
3rd Level 12 Fireball, Lightning Bolt, Dispel Magic
4th Level 18 Ice Storm, Wall of Fire, Polymorph
5th Level 25 Cone of Cold, Raise Dead, Teleportation Circle
6th Level 35 Chain Lightning, Disintegrate, Heal
7th Level 45 Finger of Death, Plane Shift, Resurrection
8th Level 60 Sunburst, Earthquake, Mind Blank
9th Level 80 Meteor Swarm, Wish, True Resurrection

Design Rationale

Lower level spells (1-3) are more affordable, allowing multiple casts per combat. Higher level spells (6-9) are expensive, limiting them to 1-2 casts per day even for high-level casters. This maintains D&D's power balance while providing AP system flexibility.

AP Recovery

  • Long Rest (8 hours): Restore all AP
  • Short Rest (1 hour): Restore 25% of max AP (once per day)
  • Meditation (Magicians only): Convert 5 HP to 5 AP (can be done multiple times, 10 minutes each)
  • Mana Potions: Restore varying amounts of AP (Minor: 10 AP, Standard: 25 AP, Greater: 50 AP)

Spell Casting Mechanics

Spell Save DC & Attack Rolls

The effectiveness of your spells is determined by your spellcasting attribute and proficiency:

Spell Save DC = 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Attribute Modifier

Spell Attack Bonus = Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Attribute Modifier

Class Spellcasting Attribute Magic Type
Wizard/Magician Intelligence (IT) Arcane/Formula Magic
Cleric Wisdom (WD) Divine Magic
Druid Wisdom (WD) Nature Magic
Warlock Intuition (IN) Pact Magic
Sorcerer Charisma (CH) Innate Magic
Bard Charisma (CH) Performance Magic
Paladin Charisma (CH) Divine Oath Magic
Ranger Wisdom (WD) Nature Magic

Example: Level 3 Wizard

Intelligence: 14 (Modifier +2)

Proficiency Bonus: +2 (Level 3)

Spell Save DC: 8 + 2 + 2 = 12

Spell Attack Bonus: +2 + 2 = +4

When casting Fireball, enemies must roll d20 + DEX save ≥ 12 to take half damage.

Spell Learning System

Classes learn spells in two ways: Prepared Casting or Known Casting.

Prepared Casters (Flexible)

These classes know all spells on their class list but must prepare a subset each day:

  • Classes: Cleric, Druid, Paladin
  • Spells Prepared: Spellcasting Modifier + Character Level
  • Change Prepared Spells: After each long rest
  • Advantage: Maximum flexibility, can adapt to any situation

Example: Level 5 Cleric

Wisdom: 15 (Modifier +2)

Spells Prepared: 2 + 5 = 7 spells

Can prepare any 7 spells from the entire Cleric spell list each day.

Known Casters (Specialized)

These classes learn specific spells permanently:

Class Spells Known (Level 1) Spells Learned Per Level
Wizard/Magician 6 spells 2 spells
Sorcerer 4 spells 1 spell
Warlock 2 spells 1 spell
Bard 4 spells 1 spell
Ranger 2 spells (Level 2+) 1 spell
  • Permanent: Once learned, spells are always available
  • Swap: Can swap 1 known spell when leveling up
  • Wizards: Can also learn spells from scrolls and spellbooks

Magician Arcane Lore Specialization

Magicians choose one school of magic at level 1:

  • Schools: Clairvoyance, Combat, Demonology, Elemental, Healing, Illusion, Metamagic, Transformation
  • Benefit: Learn +1 extra spell from chosen school per level
  • AP Discount: Spells from chosen school cost -2 AP (minimum 1 AP)
  • House Spells: 3 signature spells from chosen school that are particularly powerful

Meditation

Generally reserved for Magicians of either sex, meditation focuses a character's energy on magic power so dramatically it drains them physically. They convert life points (LP) to astral points (AP). Other magic users who carry at least one dose of thonnys may meditate as well.

Wand Spells

Available only to Magicians or Magiciennes. Allows transfer of astral points to a magical wand. The transfer consumes many astral points but is permanent once charged. Character unable to perform any magic for remainder of camp after attempting wand spell, regardless of success or failure.

Spell Origins

Every spell has an origin - a group that long ago sought the knowledge of the gods to produce a divine effect:

  • M - Magician/Magician
  • E - Elf
  • D - Druid/Druid
  • W - Warlock/Warlock

Although any character may learn and cast almost any spell, the sheer number of spells available encourages characters to specialize.

Magic-Casting Classes

Several classes have the ability to cast spells, each with their own unique approach to magic. Understanding these differences is crucial for building an effective magic user.

Primary Spellcasters

These classes are dedicated primarily to magic and have the highest spell increase attempts:

Magician & Magician

Magic Type: Formula (Book-based)

Spell Increase Attempts: 40

Approach: Scholarly and methodical. Magicians view magic as a science, meticulously recording spells in grimoires. They can specialize in Arcane Lore (Clairvoyance, Combat, Demonology, Dispell, Domination, Healing, Movement, Transformation, or Transmutation) to gain bonuses with specific house spells.

Special Abilities: Meditation (convert LP to AP), Wand Spells (permanent enchantments)

Cleric

Magic Type: Divine (Clerical)

Spell Increase Attempts: 35

Approach: Divine intermediaries who channel the power of their deity. Clerics receive magic directly from divine sources and can choose a Divine Domain (Life, War, Knowledge, etc.) that determines their specific powers.

Special Abilities: Channel Divinity, Turn Undead, Domain-specific powers

Bard

Magic Type: Arcane (Music-based)

Spell Increase Attempts: 30

Approach: Versatile performers who weave magic through music, poetry, and storytelling. Their magic is tied to performance and emotion, making it more intuitive than scholarly magic but more structured than instinctive magic.

Special Abilities: Bardic Inspiration, Jack of All Trades versatility

Hybrid Spellcasters

These classes balance magic with other abilities:

Druid & Druid

Magic Type: Dark Spells (Control)

Spell Increase Attempts: 25

Approach: Guardians of nature who command both creation and destruction. Their "dark spells" represent the controlling, destructive aspects of nature - not evil, but necessary for maintaining balance. They can command plants, call predators, and manipulate life force.

Restrictions: Cannot use metal armor or weapons

Warlock & Warlock

Magic Type: Non-book (Intuitive)

Spell Increase Attempts: 25

Approach: Intuitive magic users who derive power from innate understanding rather than bookish knowledge. Their magic flows from connection to natural forces and primal energies, manifesting through rituals, charms, and communion with spirits or otherworldly patrons.

Style: Personal and instinctive - they feel the flow of magical energy rather than understanding theoretical principles

Monk

Magic Type: Ki (Mystical Energy)

Spell Increase Attempts: 25

Approach: Masters of martial arts who harness mystical Ki energy to perform superhuman feats. Through meditation and training, they transform their bodies into living weapons.

Special Abilities: Unarmored Defense, Ki Powers (stunning strikes, supernatural speed, wall running)

Limited Spellcasters

These classes have access to magic but focus primarily on other abilities:

Paladin

Magic Type: Divine (Holy)

Spell Increase Attempts: 20

Approach: Holy warriors who combine martial prowess with divine magic. Bound by Sacred Oaths, they draw power from unwavering faith and conviction. They can channel divine energy to heal allies and smite enemies with holy power.

Special Abilities: Divine Smite, Lay on Hands, Oath-specific powers

Ranger

Magic Type: Nature (Primal)

Spell Increase Attempts: 15

Approach: Wilderness warriors who blend martial skill with primal magic. Their magic enhances natural abilities, allowing them to communicate with animals and harness nature's power in combat.

Special Abilities: Favored Enemy, Natural Explorer, tracking expertise

Note on Elves:

All Elves receive 20 spell increase attempts regardless of their class, reflecting their natural affinity for magic. This can make Elven Warriors or Rogues surprisingly capable with spells.

Combat Magic

Casting Spells in Combat

Action Economy

  • Standard Spell (1 action): Uses your entire action for the turn. You cannot attack or use skills.
  • Bonus Action Spell: Some quick spells use a bonus action, allowing you to also take a regular action.
  • Reaction Spell: Defensive spells triggered by enemy actions (e.g., Shield, Counterspell).
  • Long Casting Time: Spells requiring 1 minute or more cannot be cast during combat.

Astral Point Cost

Spells consume AP based on their power level:

Spell Level AP Cost Examples
Cantrips (Level 0) 0 AP Fire Bolt, Sacred Flame, Mage Hand
Level 1 2-4 AP Cure Wounds, Magic Missile, Shield
Level 2 5-8 AP Spiritual Weapon, Misty Step, Hold Person
Level 3 9-12 AP Fireball, Haste, Revivify
Level 4 13-16 AP Greater Invisibility, Polymorph, Wall of Fire
Level 5+ 17-45 AP See spell list for specific costs

Line of Sight

  • Must have clear line of sight to target
  • Back row casters have full visibility over front row
  • Some spells can target areas or use indirect targeting
  • Obstacles and walls block line of sight

Spell Duration

In Combat

Spell durations are converted to turns for tactical clarity:

Duration Type Combat Effect Examples
Instantaneous Immediate effect Damage spells, healing spells
1 Round Until your next turn Shield, Hellish Rebuke
1 Minute 10 turns Bless, Bane, Hunter's Mark
10 Minutes+ Lasts entire combat Aid, Mage Armor (if cast before)

Out of Combat

Spells use their listed time-based durations:

  • 1 Hour: 60 minutes of exploration
  • 8 Hours: Until next long rest
  • 24 Hours: One full day
  • Until Dispelled: Permanent until removed

Concentration

Concentration Limit

You can only maintain ONE concentration spell at a time. Casting a new concentration spell ends the previous one.

Maintaining Concentration

  • When Damaged: Make WIS saving throw (DC = 10 or half damage taken, whichever is higher)
  • If Failed: Spell ends immediately
  • If Incapacitated: Concentration automatically ends
  • Voluntary End: Can drop concentration as a free action

Concentration Spells

Marked with [Concentration] tag in spell descriptions. Common examples:

  • Buff Spells: Bless, Haste, Greater Invisibility
  • Control Spells: Hold Person, Entangle, Hypnotic Pattern
  • Summoning Spells: Conjure Animals, Spiritual Weapon
  • Area Effects: Fog Cloud, Spirit Guardians, Wall of Fire

Strategic Importance:

Concentration prevents unlimited buff stacking and creates meaningful tactical choices. Do you maintain Haste for extra attacks, or switch to Bless for better accuracy? Breaking enemy concentration becomes a valuable combat tactic.

Pre-Combat Preparation

Long-Duration Buffs

Spells lasting 1 hour or more can be cast before entering combat:

  • Still consume AP when cast
  • Provide tactical advantage going into battle
  • Examples: Mage Armor (8 hours), Aid (8 hours), Longstrider (1 hour)

Ritual Casting

Spells with the [Ritual] tag can be cast without consuming AP outside of combat:

  • Cannot be used in combat
  • Useful for exploration and utility
  • No time tracking required - simply declare ritual casting
  • Examples: Detect Magic, Identify, Alarm, Comprehend Languages

Magic Strategy

  • AP Management: Don't exhaust all astral points - keep reserve for emergencies
  • Spell Selection: Learn variety of spells for different situations
  • Specialization: Choose Arcane Lore that matches your party needs
  • Meditation Timing: Use meditation at camp when LP is high
  • Wand Charging: Invest in useful utility spells for wands
  • Combat Positioning: Keep magic users in back row for protection and visibility
  • Concentration Management: Choose your concentration spell wisely - you can only have one active
  • Pre-Combat Buffs: Cast long-duration spells before battle to save actions in combat
  • Ritual Casting: Use ritual spells freely outside combat (0 AP cost)
  • Cantrip Usage: Rely on cantrips for minor encounters to conserve AP
  • Breaking Concentration: Target enemy casters to disrupt their powerful spells
