Crafting Professions

Overview

Crafting allows adventurers to create useful items, enhance their gear, and brew powerful concoctions. Success in crafting requires the appropriate tools, raw materials, and a successful skill check.

The Crafting Process

  1. Gather Materials: Collect herbs, ores, or hides from the wilderness or shops.
  2. Equip Tools: Ensure you have the necessary tools (e.g., Alchemy Set, Hammer).
  3. Skill Check: Perform a skill check against the relevant attribute (e.g., Dexterity or Wisdom).
  4. Result: Success yields the item; failure may consume materials or produce a lower-quality item.

Alchemist

Alchemists are masters of mixing herbs, minerals, and magical essences to create potions, poisons, and transmutations. This profession is favored by Magicians and Druids.

Potions

Brew healing draughts, mana restoratives, and buff potions.

  • Healing Potion: Restores HP. Requires Wirsel Herb.
  • Mana Potion: Restores AP. Requires Whirlweed.
  • Antidote: Cures poison. Requires Thistle.

Transmutation

Convert base materials into more valuable forms or alter their properties.

  • Lead to Gold: (High Level) Convert metal bars.
  • Essence Extraction: Extract magical essence from monster parts.

Blacksmith

Blacksmiths forge metal into weapons and armor. They are essential for maintaining the party's combat readiness. Warriors and Dwarves often excel in this trade.

Forging

Create new weapons and armor from metal ingots.

  • Weapons: Swords, axes, and maces.
  • Armor: Chainmail, plate armor, and shields.
  • Customization: Add balance or weight for stat adjustments.

Maintenance

Repair and enhance existing gear.

  • Sharpening: Temporarily increase weapon damage.
  • Repair: Fix broken or damaged equipment.
  • Reinforce: Increase armor durability.

Leatherworker

Leatherworkers process hides and skins to create light armor, bags, and utility items. This profession complements Hunters and Rogues.

Tanning & Stitching

Process raw hides into usable leather.

  • Curing: Turn raw skins into leather.
  • Hardening: Boil leather for armor crafting.

Crafting

Create essential travel gear and light protection.

  • Light Armor: Leather jerkins, boots, and gloves.
  • Containers: Backpacks, belt pouches, and quivers.
  • Utility: Belts, scabbards, and straps.
