Game Options

Overview

A click of this icon gets you behind the scenes and lets you perform several game functions. Moving to the book screen, you'll see the time and date, a description of your current location, and the following game options.

Available Options

Load Game

Just like the Temple options, you can load a previously saved game using this option. Just select one from the menu of six saves.

Save Game

While out on an adventure, it is possible to save your game at nearly any time. Just select this option to do so. You will be presented with your current list of saves. Select one and rename it if you wish.

Caution: It's better to save your game at a Temple found in nearly every town or city. The game rewards players who brave it from town to town. Those who take the safe approach and save outside of Temples will lose 50 experience points each time.

Save Location Experience Point Effect
At Temple Full experience points retained
Outside Temple Lose 50 EP per save

Erase Character

Select this option to permanently remove a character from the register. You may only erase characters who are not in the party. Erased characters are gone forever.

Warning: This action is permanent and cannot be undone. Make sure the character is not in your active party before attempting to erase.

Examine Map

As your characters travel, they will come across numerous informative map fragments. To view them, select this option. The map pieces your party currently possesses are displayed and you are shown where they fit into the overall larger map.

Map Fragments:

  • Found during exploration
  • Reveal portions of the world map
  • Help with navigation and planning
  • Can be examined at any time
  • Pieces fit together to form complete map

View Diary

Your characters keep a log of their travels. You may view it any time by selecting View Diary. You'll see a listing of their travels by date, month, and location.

Diary Features:

  • Automatic logging of travels and events
  • Organized by date and location
  • Useful for tracking quest progress
  • Helps remember important events
  • Can be reviewed at any time

Adjust Performance

Adjust Performance allows you to slow down or speed up according to your system's speed. The default setting is 4. Use setting 1 for faster combat; use 10 to slow it down.

Setting Speed Best For
1 Fastest Fast systems, experienced players
4 Default/Medium Most systems, balanced gameplay
10 Slowest Older systems, tactical gameplay

Toggle Sound

When you just can't sleep but others can, this allows you to turn sound on and off.

  • Instantly mute or unmute all game sounds
  • Useful for playing in quiet environments
  • Setting persists until changed

End Game

When your day in the game world is at an end, select this option to return to the real world. This option does not save your game. Select Save Game to do so.

Important: Always save before ending game, or all unsaved progress will be lost!

Back To Game

When you've finished saving your game, toggling the sound, or otherwise, click the right mouse button to return to your adventure.

Best Practices

Saving Strategy

  • Save Often: Regular saves prevent loss of progress
  • Use Multiple Slots: Keep backup saves in case you want to try different approaches
  • Name Saves Descriptively: Use clear names to track different save points
  • Temple Saves: Wait for temples to avoid EP penalty when possible
  • Pre-Major Events: Always save before difficult battles or important decisions

Diary Usage

  • Review diary regularly to track quest progress
  • Check diary when you forget what you were supposed to do
  • Use diary entries to remember NPC names and locations
  • Reference diary for chronological understanding of events

Performance Optimization

  • Start with default setting (4) and adjust as needed
  • Lower setting if combat feels too slow
  • Raise setting if actions are too fast to follow
  • Find the sweet spot for your system and play style
