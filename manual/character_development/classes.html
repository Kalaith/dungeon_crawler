Character Classes

Overview

Character classes define your character's role in the party, their combat abilities, and their approach to challenges. Each class has unique strengths, weaknesses, and special abilities that make them valuable in different situations.

Understanding Class Requirements

Each class listing includes the following information:

  • Primary Attributes: The most important stats for this class
  • Minimum Requirements: Attribute scores needed to choose this class
  • Special Training: Unique abilities and skills the class learns
  • Magic Type: What kind of magic (if any) the class can use

Attribute Legend

  • CO - Courage (Bravery and mental fortitude)
  • IN - Intuition (Perception and insight)
  • CH - Charisma (Social influence and presence)
  • DE - Dexterity (Agility and precision)
  • AG - Agility (Speed and reflexes)
  • ST - Strength (Physical power)
  • CN - Constitution (Endurance and health)
  • IT - Intelligence (Knowledge and reasoning)

Available Classes

Jester

Requirements: IN 12, CH 13, DE 12

Special Training: Acrobatics, Disguise, Human Nature, Pickpocket, Seduce, Streetwise

Magic: None

Jesters are wanderers of the road, entertainers and tricksters who use wit, charm, and agility to survive. They excel at social manipulation, stealth, and getting into (and out of) trouble. While they lack combat prowess and magical abilities, their diverse skill set makes them invaluable for reconnaissance, negotiation, and unconventional problem-solving.

Hunter

Requirements: IN 13, DE 13, AG 13

Special Training: Camouflage, Danger Sense, Orienting, Plant Lore, Survival, Tracking

Magic: None

Hunters are masters of the wilderness, skilled trackers and survivalists who thrive in natural environments. They possess keen senses, knowledge of flora and fauna, and the ability to navigate even the most treacherous terrain. Hunters are excellent scouts and ranged combatants, though they lack magical abilities.

Warrior

Requirements: CO 13, ST 13, CN 13

Special Training: Body Control, Carouse, Feat of Strength, Intimidate, Riding, Willpower

Magic: None

Warriors are strong and hardy individuals, trained in the arts of combat and physical prowess. They serve as the frontline defenders of any party, capable of withstanding tremendous punishment while dealing devastating blows to their enemies. Members of this class excel in melee combat and can wear the heaviest armor, though they have no magical abilities.

Rogue

Requirements: CO 12, IN 12, DE 13, AG 13

Special Training: Climbing, Locks, Perception, Pick Pockets, Stealth, Traps

Magic: None

Rogues are masters of stealth, deception, and precision. They specialize in infiltration, trap detection and disarmament, and striking from the shadows. While not as durable as Warriors, Rogues make up for it with cunning, agility, and the ability to exploit enemy weaknesses. They are essential for navigating dangerous dungeons and urban environments.

Warlock

Requirements: CO 12, IN 12, CH 13, IT 13

Special Training: Fast Talk, Magical Lore, Meditate, Seduce, Treat Wounds

Magic: Warlock Magic (Pact-based, Charisma-focused)

Warlocks are practitioners of pact magic, drawing power from otherworldly patrons through bargains and agreements. Their magic is versatile and often carries a darker edge, focusing on curses, hexes, and eldritch powers. Warlocks blend social skills with magical prowess, making them effective both in and out of combat.

Magic Style: Warlock magic is characterized by its pact-based nature and reliance on Charisma. Warlocks can cast fewer spells than other casters but recover their magical energy more quickly. Their spells often involve manipulation, control, and dealing damage through eldritch forces.

Sorcerer

Requirements: CO 12, CH 13, IT 13

Special Training: Arcane Lore, Concentration, Magical Lore, Meditate, Spellcraft

Magic: Sorcerer Magic (Innate, Charisma-based)

Sorcerers are born with innate magical abilities flowing through their veins, often due to a magical bloodline or supernatural event. Unlike Wizards who study magic academically, Sorcerers channel raw magical energy instinctively. Their magic is spontaneous and powerful, though less versatile than studied spellcasting.

Magic Style: Sorcerer magic is innate and Charisma-based, allowing for powerful metamagic abilities that can modify spells on the fly. Sorcerers know fewer spells than Wizards but can cast them more flexibly, making them excellent at adapting to unexpected situations.

Wizard

Requirements: CO 12, IN 12, IT 13

Special Training: Ancient Languages, Arcane Lore, History, Magical Lore, Read/Write, Spellcraft

Magic: Wizard Magic (Studied, Intelligence-based)

Wizards are scholarly spellcasters who have mastered magic through years of study and practice. They possess the most extensive spell repertoire of any class and can learn virtually any spell given enough time and resources. Wizards are physically frail but intellectually formidable, capable of solving problems through knowledge and magical power.

Magic Style: Wizard magic is Intelligence-based and requires preparation. Wizards must study their spellbooks and prepare specific spells each day, but they have access to the largest variety of magical effects, from devastating combat spells to powerful utility magic.

Cleric

Requirements: CO 13, IN 12, CH 12, IT 12

Special Training: Etiquette, History, Meditate, Religion, Treat Poison, Treat Wounds

Magic: Divine Magic (Faith-based, Wisdom-focused)

Clerics are divine spellcasters who channel the power of their deity to heal allies, smite enemies, and provide spiritual guidance. They serve as the party's primary healers and support casters, though they can also hold their own in melee combat with divine blessings. Clerics balance martial prowess with divine magic.

Magic Style: Divine magic is granted by deities and focuses on healing, protection, and channeling divine energy. Clerics prepare spells through prayer and can turn undead creatures. Their magic is particularly effective against unholy creatures and in supporting allies.

Druid

Requirements: IN 13, CH 12, IT 12

Special Training: Animal Lore, Herbalism, Plant Lore, Survival, Treat Poison, Weather Sense

Magic: Primal Magic (Nature-based, Wisdom-focused)

Druids are guardians of nature who draw their power from the natural world. They can shapeshift into animals, command the forces of nature, and communicate with beasts. Druids are versatile spellcasters who excel in outdoor environments and can serve multiple roles within a party through their shapeshifting abilities.

Magic Style: Primal magic is drawn from nature itself and focuses on transformation, elemental forces, and natural phenomena. Druids can wild shape into various animal forms, gaining their physical abilities while retaining their mental faculties. Their spells often involve plants, animals, weather, and the elements.

Magician

Requirements: IN 13, DE 13, IT 13

Special Training: Alchemy, Arcane Lore, Magical Lore, Meditate, Spellcraft, Treat Poison

Magic: Hybrid Magic (Combines Arcane and Alchemical)

Magicians are hybrid spellcasters who blend traditional arcane magic with alchemical knowledge. They can create potions, elixirs, and magical items while also casting spells. Magicians are particularly skilled at magical crafting and understanding the theoretical underpinnings of magic, making them excellent at identifying and using magical items.

Magic Style: Magician magic combines spell casting with alchemy and item creation. They can prepare both spells and alchemical concoctions, giving them unique versatility. Their magic often involves transmutation, enhancement, and the creation of temporary magical effects through alchemical means.

Party Composition Considerations

A well-balanced party typically includes a mix of combat specialists, support characters, and utility experts. Consider including:

  • Frontline Combatants: Warriors, Clerics (1-2 characters)
  • Ranged Attackers: Hunters, Rogues, Wizards (1-2 characters)
  • Magical Support: Clerics, Druids, Warlocks (1-2 characters)
  • Utility Specialists: Rogues, Jesters, Magicians (1 character)

Note: Parties can have up to 6 members. The specific composition should be tailored to your preferred playstyle and the challenges you expect to face.

Advanced Mode Benefits

When playing in Advanced Mode, certain classes gain additional benefits and options:

  • Wizards: Can learn spells from scrolls and other spellbooks
  • Clerics: Gain access to domain-specific powers based on their deity
  • Druids: Can wild shape into additional creature types
  • Magicians: Can create more powerful alchemical items and magical equipment
  • Rogues: Gain expertise in additional skills
  • Warlocks: Can invoke additional eldritch powers from their patron

Multiclassing

Characters may choose to train in multiple classes, gaining abilities from each. However, multiclassing requires meeting the attribute requirements for all chosen classes and may slow overall progression. Consult with your Game Master about multiclassing rules and restrictions.
