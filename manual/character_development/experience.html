Experience & Leveling

Gaining Levels

As your characters travel, they will gain experience points as they face numerous foes, perform herculean tasks, and overcome other perils. When your characters accumulate enough experience points, they become eligible for new experience levels. These levels, along with all their benefits, are granted in towns.

Experience Point Requirements

There is no maximum level in the game. Experience requirements follow a progressive formula, allowing characters to grow indefinitely as long as they continue their adventures.

The Experience Formula

The experience points (EP) required to reach a specific level (L) are calculated as follows:

EP = 5000 × (L - 1) × L / 2

Where L is the target level.

For example, to reach Level 4:

  • L = 4
  • EP = 5000 × (3) × 4 / 2
  • EP = 5000 × 12 / 2
  • EP = 30,000

Level-Up Benefits

When your character gains a level, they will enjoy numerous benefits:

Attribute Improvements

You may choose to increase any positive attribute by 1 point, followed by a decrease of 2 points to your negative attributes.

  • Select one positive attribute to increase
  • Decrease negative attributes by a total of 2 points
  • Follow the same trading rules as character creation

Life Points Increase

Character Type LP Increase
Non-Magic Users Up to 6 points
Magic Users Up to 8 points (can be divided between LP and AP)

Magic Users: May divide the 8 points between Life Points (LP) and Astral Points (AP) as desired.

Advanced Mode Level-Up

If you are playing in the Advanced mode, you will have the opportunity to use skill and spell increase attempts to continue shaping the ideal character, much as you may have done in Advanced Character Generation.

Skill Increase Attempts

Class Skill Attempts
Most Archetypes 20 attempts
Magician and Magician 15 attempts

Skill Increase Rules:

  • When increasing combat skills, you will have the opportunity to adjust AT and PA values
  • Follow the same skill increase limits as character creation
  • Combat and Intuitive skills: Max +1 per level
  • Knowledge skills: Max +3 per level
  • All other skills: Max +2 per level

Spell Increase Attempts

Spell increase attempts depend on Class:

Class Spell Attempts
Magician and Magician 40 attempts
Druid, Druid, Warlock, Warlock 25 attempts
All Elves 20 attempts

Conversion Options

Skill and Spell Conversion:

With the exception of Elves, magic users may convert between skill increase attempts and spell increase attempts:

  • Convert skill increase attempts into spell increase attempts
  • Convert spell increase attempts into skill increase attempts

Astral Point Conversion (Magicians and Magiciennes Only):

Magicians and Magiciennes may forgo some spell increase attempts in return for astral points.

  • Trade spell increase attempts for permanent AP increases
  • Strategic choice between immediate power (AP) and long-term versatility (spell increases)

Level-Up Process

  1. Accumulate Experience: Gain EP through combat, quests, and overcoming challenges
  2. Reach Town: Levels are granted only in towns where citizens recognize your deeds
  3. Level Up: Character automatically levels when EP threshold is reached in town
  4. Attribute Increase: Choose one positive attribute to increase (decrease 2 negative points)
  5. Life/Astral Points: Receive LP increase (and possibly AP for magic users)
  6. Advanced Mode Only: Use skill and spell increase attempts
  7. Save Game: It's a good idea to save your game at the nearest temple after gaining a level

Important: Levels are granted in towns only. Creatures in the wilderness do not recognize your achievements. Plan your journeys to ensure you can reach a town when close to leveling up.

Strategic Considerations

Attribute Choices

When choosing which positive attribute to increase, consider:

  • Your character's role in the party
  • Weaknesses that need shoring up
  • Strengths that could be enhanced
  • Requirements for future Class abilities
  • Which negative attributes you can afford to increase

Skill vs. Spell Priorities

For magic users in Advanced mode, decide between:

  • Spell Focus: Use all attempts on spells for powerful magic
  • Balanced Approach: Split between skills and spells for versatility
  • Skill Focus: Prioritize non-magical abilities for a hybrid character
  • AP Investment: Trade spell attempts for permanent astral power

Combat Skill Development

When increasing combat skills, you can adjust AT and PA values:

  • Offensive Build: Maximize AT for aggressive combat style
  • Defensive Build: Maximize PA for survivability
  • Balanced Build: Split evenly between AT and PA
