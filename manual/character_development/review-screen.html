Review Screen

Overview

After you've rounded up your party and want to review one of your characters (just double click on their portrait), you'll notice that they have several new attributes that weren't apparent during character generation. Additionally, when you review your characters, you'll also have new options available to you.

The character review screen shows you everything you need to know about your character, and also allows for their feeding and care. Nearly every section of the review screen offers some kind of information.

The Value Of Statistics

When you review your characters, you'll notice that their statistics have two values: a base value and the current value.

Value Type Position Description
Base Value Right The statistic's normal level. This value changes only when characters gain a level. It serves as a reminder of the actual value's peak level.
Current Value Left The current level of the statistic that changes as characters become hurt, suffer a malady, or are influenced by magic, potions, or herbs.

As characters travel, the values for certain attributes may change. When a character is hurt by a monster, for instance, the actual value of LP (life points) will decrease. Other times, attributes are affected. Charisma, for example, goes down when characters become intoxicated.

Examining Items

To look at an item in your character's possession, click on it followed by the eye icon. You will be told what it is and what condition it is in.

Items In The Backpack

Every character has a backpack, a place where they can store up to 16 items. By left clicking on an item to select it, it highlights red. Characters can drop it, give it to another player, or use it in some other way.

Backpack Management:

  • Capacity: 16 items maximum
  • Select items by left clicking (highlights red)
  • Selected items can be dropped, given, or used
  • Items in backpack are not immediately accessible in combat

Items On The Body - Equipping Characters

In addition to their backpacks, characters may carry seven items on their body. These items are considered "equipped" and at the ready. Obviously, a sword in the backpack won't be nearly as useful in combat as a sword in the hand.

Equipment Slots

Each of the seven carrying slots represents a part of the body:

  • Head: Helmets, hats, caps
  • Torso: Armor, shirts, robes
  • Foot: Boots, shoes
  • Right Hand: Primary weapon
  • Left Hand: Shield, secondary weapon, or items for quick use
  • Other slots: Various armor pieces and accessories

Note: Items that cover parts of the body work to improve Armor Class (AR). To equip your character, select an item from inventory and drag it to the appropriate body icon. If you try to equip something your character can't wear, the system will let you know.

The Left & Right Hand

While most of the body icons are used for armor and clothing, the left and right hand icons have special significance. Only items in those two boxes can be used instantly.

Hand Purpose Items Combat Importance
Right Hand Weapon Hand Primary weapon This weapon will be at the ready should a monster approach. If characters don't have a weapon here and are attacked, they will have to arm themselves in the first round, losing precious time, or fight unarmed.
Left Hand Quick Access Potions, herbs, shields, or missiles Items in the left hand are held ready for use at a moment's notice during combat.

Review Options

While reviewing your character, you have numerous options aside from equipping them or investigating their basic statistics. To see the options available to you, click the right mouse button. You'll see a menu box.

Available Options

Option Description Usage
Change Name Rename your character Select this option to change your character's name at any time.
Use Object Use an item Some items can be used, like using a tinder box to light a torch or using a scroll by reading it. Select the item followed by Use Object. If you select an item which can't be used, the system will let you know.
Drop Object Discard an item If you're hoping to lower encumbrance or if your character is carrying a lot of junk, get rid of it using Drop Object. First, select the object you wish to drop, then select Drop Object. Items that are dropped are gone forever. The system will ask for confirmation before the item disappears.
Sickness View disease information This option will only appear on the menu if the character is ill. After selecting it, you will receive information about the character's condition.
Poisoning View poison information This option will only appear on the menu if the character is actually poisoned. After selecting it, you will receive information about the character's condition.
Use Talent Use a skill Certain skills may be used while reviewing your character. Generally, these skills are of the healing variety (Treat Wounds, for example). To use any of the skills available, just select it.
Cast Spell Cast a spell To have your character cast a spell, select Cast Spell. You will be asked to choose the spell group, followed by the actual spell. In general, healing or adventure type spells (like light spell) are the type you would cast in review.
ATT/PARRY Values Toggle combat values Toggle between the character's basic values and Attack and Parry values. Advanced mode only.
Skills Review skills Review a character's skills. Advanced mode only.
Magic Skills Review spells Review your character's magical proficiency. Advanced mode only.
Exit Close review screen Leave the character's review and return to the Main Screen.

Spell Casting Safety: Detonating a fire ball into empty air could prove rather hazardous, not to mention a waste of astral points. Stick to healing or utility spells when using Cast Spell from the review screen.

Character Management Tips

Regular Review Habits:

  • Check encumbrance regularly to ensure optimal movement points
  • Monitor hunger and thirst levels
  • Keep right hand equipped with your best weapon
  • Keep left hand ready with healing potion or shield
  • Review current vs. base values to check for status effects
  • Treat wounds and diseases promptly
  • Reorganize inventory to drop unnecessary items

Equipment Best Practices:

  • Always have a weapon in right hand before adventuring
  • Equip armor in all available slots for maximum protection
  • Balance encumbrance with protection needs
  • Keep essential items in left hand for quick combat access
  • Store backup equipment in backpack
  • Examine items before use to understand their properties
