Derived Statistics

Overview

Derived statistics are calculated from your character's primary attributes and Class. These statistics play crucial roles in gameplay, affecting everything from survival to combat effectiveness to magical ability.

Primary Derived Statistics

Health Points (HP)

Represent the amount of damage a character can take before death. A character's Health Points are based upon their statistics and increase with experience.

Health Points Mechanics:

  • Based on character's Constitution (CO) and Strength (ST)
  • Increase with experience
  • At 5 or fewer HP: Character will lose consciousness
  • At 0 HP: Character dies
  • Recovery: Health Points can be regained through sleep, potions, or magical intervention

Astral Points (AP)

All kinds of magic are powered by astral energy which is measured in astral points. These points represent the amount of magical power your character has and the amount of points available to cast spells.

Astral Points Mechanics:

  • Based upon character's Class
  • Increase with experience
  • Recovery: Replenished through sleep, potions, or meditation
  • Each spell cast consumes a specific amount of AP

Endurance

Reflects your character's ability to withstand strain and is a key factor in how much burden can be tolerated before suffering exhaustion or penalties.

Endurance Calculation:
Based on Health Points (HP) + Constitution (CO)

Magic Resistance (MR)

Represents a character's ability to ward off the effects of magical spells.

Magic Resistance Calculation:
Based on Courage (CR) + Wisdom (WD) + Superstition (SN)

Secondary Derived Statistics

Once characters are generated and have joined your party, they acquire three new secondary statistics, each of which are seen in the character review.

Encumbrance (ENC)

A character can only carry so much before their knees start to buckle and back gives out. Encumbrance tracks this load, and tells you how many ounces of armor, weapons, and supplies a character carries.

Encumbrance Rules:

  • Maximum Capacity: 100 × Strength (ST) ounces
  • Characters who push the limit or exceed it suffer penalties to Initiative and physical checks
  • Affects how quickly a character can react in combat
  • Tracks total weight of armor, weapons, and supplies

Maximum Encumbrance:
100 × ST (in ounces)

Initiative (Speed)

Initiative determines the order in which characters and enemies act during combat. A higher initiative means acting earlier in the round, allowing you to strike first, heal allies before they fall, or control the battlefield.

Initiative Mechanics:

  • Calculation: d20 + AG Modifier + IN Modifier (if IN ≥ 12)
  • Agility (AG): Primary initiative attribute (reaction speed)
  • Intuition (IN): Adds to initiative if 12+ (danger sense)
  • Turn Order: Highest to Lowest
  • Ties: Resolved by higher AG, then higher IN, then simultaneous
  • Surprise: Surprised creatures cannot act on the first round

Initiative Examples:

Character 1: AG 10 (+0), IN 8 (+0) → Initiative = d20 + 0

Character 2: AG 12 (+1), IN 13 (+1) → Initiative = d20 + 2

Character 3: AG 13 (+1), IN 10 (+0) → Initiative = d20 + 1 (IN below 12)

Movement Points (Overworld)

While combat movement is abstract (Front/Back rows), traveling the overworld requires Movement Points.

Movement Mechanics:

  • Daily Pool: Characters/Parties have a set number of Movement Points per day based on their mode of travel (Foot, Ship, etc.).
  • Terrain Cost: Difficult terrain (swamps, mountains) costs more points to traverse.
  • Exhaustion: Pushing beyond your daily limit causes exhaustion.

Armor Class (AC)

A character's Armor Class represents how hard it is for enemies to land a solid, damaging blow. It is a combination of physical armor, agility, and magical protection.

AC Value Protection Level
10 Unarmored (Base)
11-13 Light Armor (Leather, Studded Leather)
14-16 Medium Armor (Scale Mail, Breastplate)
18-20+ Heavy Armor (Plate) & Shields

Armor Class Mechanics:

  • Base: 10 + Dexterity Modifier (unless wearing heavy armor)
  • Higher is Better: Enemies must roll equal to or higher than your AC to hit.
  • Shields: Typically add +2 to AC.
  • Magic: Spells like Mage Armor or Shield can temporarily boost AC.

Currency System

Money is an important commodity in the game world. This statistic shows the amount of money your character currently has.

Currency Symbol Conversion
Gold Ducats D Highest form of currency
Silver Crowns S 10 Silver Crowns = 1 Gold Ducat
Copper Bits B 10 Copper Bits = 1 Silver Crown

Currency Example:

You may see, for instance, "10D 2S 5B" on your character's screen, meaning they have:

  • 10 Gold Ducats
  • 2 Silver Crowns
  • 5 Copper Bits

Your character's make up and a bit of luck determine how much money they receive at creation.

Physical Characteristics

Height and Weight

Help to define the physical aspects of your character and are based on the Class you selected for your character.

Deity

A character's deity is very important. Based upon the month the character is born, the deity determines the character's general way of life and may offer bonuses to attribute or skill values.

Deity Benefits:

  • Determines character's general way of life
  • May offer bonuses to attribute or skill values
  • Temples throughout the world offer respite for travelers
  • Characters in need may enjoy divine help
  • Steady worship and sacrifice will make a favorable impression
