Attributes

Overview

Characters have certain levels of wisdom, dexterity, and strength among other statistics. Attributes are split into two categories: positive attributes and negative attributes. Both types are tracked numerically and affect various aspects of gameplay.

Positive Attributes

The eight positive attributes are tracked numerically and, for newly created characters, range from 8 to 13.

Attribute Code Range Description
Courage CR 8-13 Represents willpower, mental fortitude, and bravery. Valuable in combat, when trying to resist fear or mind-affecting spells, and for maintaining resolve in difficult conversations.
Wisdom WD 8-13 Reflects how attuned you are to the world around you, representing perceptiveness, insight, and intuition. It helps in reading body language, noticing environmental details, and is the basis for nature-based magic (Druids).
Intelligence IT 8-13 Measures mental acuity, accuracy of recall, and the ability to reason. It represents logic, education, memory, and deductive reasoning. This is the primary attribute for "bookish" or formula-based magic (Magicians).
Charisma CH 8-13 Measures your ability to interact effectively with others. It includes factors such as confidence, eloquence, and commanding personality. High charisma makes it easier to influence others and lead the party.
Dexterity DX 8-13 Measures fine motor skills and hand-eye coordination. Essential for manipulating small objects, disarming traps, opening locks, and performing the complex gestures required for spell weaving.
Agility AG 8-13 Measures reflexes, balance, and bodily coordination. Crucial for dodging blows in combat, moving stealthily, and performing acrobatic maneuvers to avoid obstacles.
Strength ST 8-13 Measures bodily power, athletic training, and raw physical force. Necessary for moving heavy objects, using heavy weapons effectively, and determines a character's maximum carrying capacity.
Constitution CO 8-13 Measures health, stamina, and vital force. Determines Hit Points and resistance to poison, disease, and fatigue.
Intuition IN 8-13 A "sixth sense" or gut feeling that warns of danger before it happens. It represents subconscious processing and luck. This attribute is vital for intuitive magic users (Warlocks, Witches, Elves) who rely on feeling rather than formula.

Attribute Modifiers

Each attribute generates a modifier used for skill checks, attack rolls, saving throws, and other game mechanics. This bridges RoA's attribute system with D&D's resolution mechanics.

Modifier Formula:
Modifier = (Attribute - 10) / 2 (rounded down)

Attribute Value Modifier Attribute Value Modifier
8-9 +0 14-15 +2
10-11 +0 16-17 +3
12-13 +1 18-19 +4

Note: Starting characters have attributes 8-13 (modifiers +0 to +1). As characters level up, attributes can increase beyond 13, providing higher modifiers.

Derived Stats

Derived statistics like Health Points (HP), Astral Points (AP), and Armor Class (AC) are calculated directly from these RoA attributes and their modifiers. Monsters also use this same attribute scale, though powerful creatures like Dragons may exceed the standard 8-13 range.

Saving Throws

When you need to resist effects (poison, spells, traps), you make a saving throw using the appropriate attribute modifier:

  • Strength Saves: Use ST modifier (resisting being pushed, grappled)
  • Dexterity Saves: Use AG modifier (dodging fireballs, avoiding traps)
  • Constitution Saves: Use CO modifier (resisting poison, disease, death)
  • Intelligence Saves: Use IT modifier (resisting mental illusions)
  • Wisdom Saves: Use WD modifier (resisting charms, fear)
  • Charisma Saves: Use CH modifier (resisting possession, banishment)

Magic System (Hybrid)

Spells use Astral Points (AP) as the resource pool. The spell's effectiveness is determined by your Spell Save DC and Spell Attack Bonus, which are calculated from your spellcasting attribute (Intelligence for Wizards, Wisdom for Clerics, etc.).

Negative Attributes

The seven negative attributes range from 2 to 8 for new characters. Archetypes have negative attribute requirements as well.

Attribute Code Range Description
Superstition SN 2-8 Characters with high superstition notice certain omens and instantly understand their meaning. High superstition often makes a character particularly susceptible to magic.
Acrophobia AC 2-8 Fear of heights, leading to a distinct feeling of vertigo. A large acrophobia value can be a severe handicap when forced to cross a suspension bridge or stand at a cliff edge.
Claustrophobia CL 2-8 Fear of being buried alive which can close in when traveling through narrow halls and low corridors.
Avarice AV 2-8 The urge to gather gold, jewelry, and other precious items. A character gripped by avarice will often forget all else happening around them just to loot something that has caught their eye.
Necrophobia NE 2-8 A particularly terrifying fear of death and anything connected with it, especially the undead, mummies, zombies, and skeletons. A character with high necrophobia will sometimes refuse to fight against undead and may run away instead.
Curiosity CU 2-8 A healthy amount of curiosity is a prerequisite for a true hero, but too much is a curse. Magicians especially are often overcome with curiosity upon discovering old scrolls and magical artifacts.
Violent Temper VT 2-8 A character with a violent temper is easily insulted and quick to draw a weapon to right any perceived wrong.

Negative Attribute Mechanical Effects

Negative attributes have real mechanical consequences when they reach certain thresholds. Effects trigger when the attribute value is 6 or higher.

Attribute Trigger Save Failure Effect
Superstition (SN) Facing illusion/enchantment spells - Disadvantage on saves vs. those spells
Acrophobia (AC) At heights (10+ feet) WD DC 15 Frightened condition
Claustrophobia (CL) In tight/enclosed spaces WD DC 15 Frightened condition
Avarice (AV) Seeing valuable treasure WD DC 15 Must attempt to take it (even if dangerous)
Necrophobia (NE) Facing undead creatures CR DC 15 Frightened of undead (cannot move closer)
Curiosity (CU) Encountering mysteries/puzzles WD DC 15 Must investigate (even if dangerous)
Violent Temper (VT) Being insulted or provoked WD DC 15 Must attack the provoker

Frightened Condition

  • Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while source of fear is visible
  • Cannot willingly move closer to the source of fear
  • Lasts until you leave the triggering situation or succeed on a save (can retry each turn)

Magic Resistance Bonus: CR Modifier + WD Modifier - (SN - 5)

This bonus applies to saving throws against magic. Higher Superstition (SN) reduces your resistance, as intended.

Example: CR 12 (+1), WD 13 (+1), SN 3: Bonus = +1 +1 - (-2) = +4 to magic saves

Attribute Assignment During Character Creation

Rolling and Placing Attributes

When creating a character, you'll see an initial roll on your screen. This roll can be used for any of the eight positive attributes; you select which one depending on the Class you're hoping to create. After placing the first value, the system will roll again until you have placed each roll with a positive statistic.

Next, you'll assign values for the negative statistics just like you did for positive attributes. Keep in mind that Archetypes have negative attribute requirements as well.

Selecting Class

After assigning your character's attribute values, choose Select Class. The system will show you a list of Archetypes your character qualifies for. Just select one to make it official.

At times, however, you may see that the character you've created doesn't qualify for any Class at all. The system will tell you that you've failed to meet the minimum entrance requirements. You don't need to start over - select the Change Attribute option to modify current values, or select New Attributes to re-roll.

Change Attributes System

When you've placed all of your rolls and are still just one or two points away from your desired Class, Change Attributes allows you to alter both positive and negative attributes by trading off points between the two.

How Trading Works

Increasing Positive Attributes

For every 1 point increase to a positive attribute, you must make a 2 point increase to negative attributes.

  • Select the positive attribute to increase
  • The system shows you a menu of negative attributes
  • Choose to increase your negative attributes by 2 points total
  • You may put both increases in one attribute or split them between two different attributes

Decreasing Negative Attributes

You can decrease 2 points from your negative attributes by choosing to remove 1 point from a positive attribute.

Trading Restrictions

Restriction Details
Positive Attribute Range Cannot be decreased below 8 or increased above 13
Negative Attribute Range Cannot go below 2 or above 8
Direction Lock Once you begin to change an attribute in one direction, you may not reverse it. Values that are lowered may not be raised later. Values that are raised may not be lowered.
Continued Adjustment You may continue heading in the same direction with multiple changes
Class Requirements Be careful not to alter attributes above or below the minimum entrance requirements for the Class you're hoping to create

Note: When you have completed the adjustment to your character's negative attributes, the system will return you to the character screen. If you have further changes, select Change Attributes again.

Attribute Impact on Gameplay

Positive Attributes Affect:

  • Courage: Combat resolve, resisting fear, social fortitude
  • Wisdom: Perception, insight, nature magic (Druids), healing
  • Intelligence: Logic, memory, formula magic (Magicians), lore knowledge
  • Charisma: Persuasion, leadership, deception, intimidation
  • Dexterity: Fine manipulation, lockpicking, spell gestures
  • Agility: Evasion, stealth, acrobatics, balance
  • Strength: Melee damage, carrying capacity, athletics
  • Constitution: Health Points (HP), disease/poison resistance, endurance
  • Intuition: Luck, sensing danger, intuitive magic (Warlocks/Elves)

Negative Attributes Affect:

  • Superstition: Omen recognition, magic susceptibility
  • Acrophobia: Performance at heights, bridge crossing, cliff edges
  • Claustrophobia: Performance in narrow spaces, low corridors
  • Avarice: Focus when treasure is present, looting behavior
  • Necrophobia: Combat against undead, death-related situations
  • Curiosity: Behavior around scrolls, artifacts, unknowns
  • Violent Temper: Response to insults, conflict escalation
