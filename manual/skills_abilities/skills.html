Skills System

Overview

Characters need certain skills to survive in their sometimes friendly, often times hostile environment. Skills are broken down into seven groups which all Archetypes have the ability to develop: Combat, Body, Social, Nature, Lore, Craftsmanship, and Intuition.

Skill Range

Skills range from -20 to +18. Those on the lower end would have trouble with even simple tasks, while those on the high side could perform complex actions with ease.

How Skills Work

Skill Check Mechanic

When your character attempts an action that requires a skill, you make a skill check using the following formula:

Skill Check = d20 + Attribute Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if trained)

Proficiency Bonus

Characters add their proficiency bonus to skills they are trained in based on their class:

Character Level Proficiency Bonus
1-4 +2
5-8 +3
9-12 +4
13-16 +5
17-20 +6

Difficulty Classes (DC)

The Game Master (or game system) sets a Difficulty Class for each task. Your skill check must meet or exceed the DC to succeed.

Difficulty DC Example
Very Easy 5 Climb a rope with knots
Easy 10 Climb a rough stone wall
Medium 15 Pick a simple lock
Hard 20 Pick a complex lock
Very Hard 25 Disarm a magical trap
Nearly Impossible 30 Convince a dragon to leave peacefully

Advantage from Secondary Attributes

Each skill lists a primary attribute and one or more secondary attributes. If your secondary attribute modifier is +2 or higher, you roll with Advantage (roll 2d20, take the higher result).

Example: Acrobatics Check

Skill: Acrobatics (AG primary, CR/ST secondary)

Character: AG 12 (+1), CR 13 (+1), ST 10 (+0), Level 3 (Proficiency +2), trained in Acrobatics

Check: d20 + 1 (AG) + 2 (proficiency) = d20 + 3

Advantage: No (CR and ST modifiers are below +2)

To succeed at DC 15: Must roll 12 or higher on d20

Example 2: Same Character at Level 5

Character: AG 14 (+2), CR 15 (+2), ST 12 (+1), Level 5 (Proficiency +3)

Check: d20 + 2 (AG) + 3 (proficiency) = d20 + 5

Advantage: Yes! (CR +2 grants advantage - roll 2d20, take higher)

To succeed at DC 15: Must roll 10 or higher (much easier with advantage)

Increasing Skill Values

In Advanced mode, characters are given a set of statistic values that reflect standard training for their Class. You receive remaining attempts (typically 20, varies by Class) to increase skills you consider important.

To Attempt a Skill Increase:

  1. Move to the skill screen that contains the skill you want to increase
  2. Open the Skill Increase Menu
  3. Select a skill group
  4. Choose a skill to increase - the system rolls a die to determine success or failure
  5. Repeat until all skill attempts are used

Skill Increase Limits

Skill Type Max Increase per Level
Combat Skills +1 point
Intuitive Skills +1 point
Knowledge (Lore) Skills +3 points
All Other Skills +2 points

Additional Rules:

  • Combat skills provide bonuses to attack rolls with specific weapon types
  • You have 3 attempts available to increase any one skill
  • Highest skill value possible: +18
  • Lowest skill value possible: -20

Combat Skills

Combat skills determine how well a character wields different weapon types in battle. These skills provide bonuses to attack rolls. Defense is handled by your Armor Class (AC), not parry values. On reaching a new level, Combat skills can be raised by 1 point.

Skill Description
Unarmed Encompasses boxing, wrestling, and kicking. A skill to fall back on in case the character's weapon breaks.
Cutting Weapons Governs use of all weapons used in a striking fashion, including edged weapons (sabre, cutlass) and some blunt weapons (mace, morning star).
Pointed Weapons Includes epee, rapier, foil, as well as all knives and daggers.
Swords Swords are used as striking weapons but can be used to pierce as well. Handled easily and readily available.
Axes Axes and hatchets are favored weapons of northern warriors and are easy to find. Do a lot of damage but require a lot of skill to handle.
Pole Arms Used for spears and pole arms, but also for quarterstaves and double fleurs.
Two-Handed Swords Governs all two-handed swords. Normally, only warriors learn to handle these awkward weapons.
Missile Weapons Most common types are the various types of bows and the crossbow.
Throwing Weapons Includes all javelins, throwing knives, throwing stars, and throwing hatchets.

Body Skills

Body skills regulate all athletic actions such as climbing, hiding, or riding. For a character to succeed in an action that requires a Body skill, good values for strength and agility are a must. When characters gain an experience level, Body skills may be increased by up to 2 points.

Skill Attributes Description
Acrobatics CR/AG/ST Covers attempts to stay on your feet in tricky situations, such as running across ice, balancing on a tightrope, or staying upright on a rocking ship's deck. Also includes performing acrobatic stunts like dives, rolls, somersaults, cartwheels, and flips for an audience. Acrobatic reactions to unexpected situations rely more on Physical Control skill.
Climb CR/AG/ST Climbing is always risky, especially at great heights. Success depends on skill level and whether specialized equipment is used.
Physical Control CR/IN/AG Allows reducing consequences of a fall, escaping an opponent's grip, or squeezing through a narrow crack.
Ride CH/AG/ST Ability to ride horseback during daring maneuvers without falling.
Stealth CR/IN/AG Ability to move without a sound and conceal yourself from enemies. Used when attempting to slink past guards, slip away without being noticed, or sneak up on someone without being seen or heard. Success depends on ground type and armor worn - noise can alert potential targets.
Swim CR/AG/ST Determines how long a character can keep their head above water. Any kind of armor is a large hindrance.
Self Control CR/ST/ST Ability to take damage without letting pain get the better of you. Allows continuing actions with accuracy and skill.
Dance CH/AG/AG Like Acrobatics, offers opportunities for earning money performing for an appreciative audience.
Hide CR/IN/AG Ability to find a hiding place quickly and quietly.
Carouse WD/IN/ST Ability to hold oneself in check while visiting taverns. Affects amount of alcohol a character can drink before becoming intoxicated.

Social Skills

Social skills help (or hinder) characters in the social situations they encounter in towns and cities. Social skills aren't much use in the wild. When characters gain a level, Social skills may be increased by up to 2 points.

Skill Attributes Description
Convert WD/IN/CH Ability to influence others with tact, social graces, and good nature to convince them your point of view is correct, especially regarding religious matters. Used when acting in good faith to foster friendships, make cordial requests, or exhibit proper etiquette. Examples include convincing a chamberlain to let your party see the king or inspiring a crowd of townsfolk.
Seduce IN/CH/CH Ability to attract and charm others through personal magnetism and social grace. Used to gain favor, secure assistance, or obtain information through romantic or social appeal. Success depends on reading social cues and presenting yourself in an appealing manner.
Haggle CR/WD/CH Good hagglers can get discounts of up to 50% from merchants in markets.
Streetwise WD/IN/CH Helps find your way in a strange town, find unguarded shortcuts, avoid city guards, and locate good begging spots.
Lie CR/IN/CH Determines whether you can convincingly hide the truth, either verbally or through your actions. This deception can encompass everything from misleading others through ambiguity to telling outright lies. Typical situations include trying to fast-talk a guard, con a merchant, pass yourself off in a disguise, dull someone's suspicions with false assurances, or maintain a straight face while telling a blatant lie.
Human Nature WD/IN/CH Allows determining the true intentions of a creature, such as when searching out a lie or predicting someone's next move. Involves gleaning clues from body language, speech habits, and changes in mannerisms. Also helps judge the probable reaction of non-player characters in your party.
Evaluate WD/IN/IN Helps characters estimate the value of artifacts and other useful items.

Nature Skills

Nature skills are a must for survival in the wilderness. They help characters feel at home with the land and are generally used whenever a party ventures outside of town. When characters gain levels, these skills may be increased by up to 2 points.

Skill Attributes Description
Track WD/IN/AG Used to follow tracks, identify the species that left them, and determine how recently they passed. Essential for hunting wild game and tracking enemies or prey through various terrain types.
Bind DX/AG/ST Knowledge of ropes and knots. Helps when tying game and freeing oneself if bound and gagged.
Orientation WD/IN/IN Well-honed sense of direction useful in wilderness and down in dungeons.
Herb Lore WD/IN/DX Measures ability to recall lore about terrain, plants and their medicinal properties, and natural cycles. Includes knowledge of where to find specific herbs, ability to correctly identify them in the wild, and skill in preparing them for various uses.
Animal Lore CR/WD/IN Comprehensive knowledge of animals including their behavior, habitats, and anatomy. Helps calm domesticated animals, keep mounts from getting spooked, intuit an animal's intentions, and control your mount during risky maneuvers. In combat, knowing vulnerable spots can mean the difference between life and death.
Survival IN/DX/AG Essential wilderness skill used to guide your group through harsh terrain, identify signs that dangerous creatures live nearby, predict the weather, and avoid natural hazards like quicksand. Also needed to find good spots for setting up camp, locate sources of water, and identify rich hunting grounds.

Lore Skills

Almost all knowledge can be covered by a Lore skill, and in many cases, Lore skills define a character's art. When characters gain new levels, Lore skills may be increased by up to 3 points.

Skill Attributes Description
Alchemy CR/WD/DX Governs recognition and preparation of potions and elixirs.
Ancient Tongues WD/WD/IN Allows identifying ancient scrolls.
Geography WD/WD/IN Lore of far-off countries.
History WD/WD/IN Measures ability to recall lore about historical events, legendary people, ancient kingdoms, past disputes, recent wars, and lost civilizations. Includes knowledge of both oral traditions and written historical records.
Ritual WD/IN/CH Measures ability to recall lore about deities, rites and prayers, religious hierarchies, holy symbols, and the practices of secret cults. Essential for understanding and performing the various rituals connected with different faiths.
Tactics CR/WD/CH Knowledge of successful combat movement.
Read/Write WD/WD/DX Ability to read and write your native tongue. Quite a rare skill.
Arcane Lore WD/WD/DX Measures ability to recall lore about spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, magical traditions, the planes of existence, and the inhabitants of those planes. Allows recognizing artifacts, judging a spell's effect, and understanding magical phenomena. An absolute must for magic users.
Tongues WD/IN/CH Determines how many foreign languages a character speaks and how well they speak them.

Craftsmanship Skills

Craftsmanship skills are based on a character's ability to influence situations or people with their hands and mind. All Craftsmanship skills are honed by constant use and practice. On reaching a new level, these skills may be increased by up to 2 points.

Skill Attributes Description
Train Animals CR/IN/CH Used to tame and train animals such as horses, hounds, or falcons.
Drive IN/CH/DX Skill of handling carts, coaches, and sleds.
Cheat CR/CH/DX Skill in manipulating games of chance through sleight of hand and misdirection. Includes using marked cards, loaded dice, palming coins, and other deceptive techniques. Promises substantial monetary rewards as long as no one notices your tricks. If caught, expect little mercy from angry gamblers and tavern keepers.
Treat Disease CR/WD/CH Essential healing skill that allows you to diagnose illnesses and treat diseased companions. Helps identify symptoms, determine the nature of the affliction, and apply appropriate remedies. Team members should be proficient with this skill.
Treat Poison CR/WD/IN Essential healing skill for dealing with poisoned characters.
Treat Wounds WD/CH/DX Very commonly used skill for adventurers. Allows you to stabilize dying companions, bind wounds, and provide emergency medical care. Success can prevent further deterioration and speed recovery. Untreated wounds often lead to infection and disease, making this skill essential for survival.
Instrument WD/IN/DX Determines how well you can delight an audience with music. With an appropriate instrument, you can earn money performing at taverns and festivals - provided you hit the right notes and the mood is right. Can also be used to boost morale or create distractions.
Locks IN/DX/DX Essential skill for survival in towns and dungeons. Covers picking locks on doors, chests, and other secured containers. Also includes understanding lock mechanisms, identifying trap triggers, and knowing which tools work best for different lock types. Success often means the difference between treasure and empty hands.
Pickpocket CR/IN/DX Covers acts of legerdemain and manual trickery, such as planting something on someone else, concealing an object on your person, lifting a coin purse, or slipping something out of another person's pocket. A risky endeavor with promise of silver or gold... or a pummeling if caught. Requires quick hands and keen awareness of your mark's attention.

Intuitive Skills

Intuitive skills are not trained but improved primarily through practice. They are the character's sixth sense. Because of this, they can only be improved by 1 point when characters gain a level.

Skill Attributes Description
Danger Sense WD/IN/IN Provides warning of immediate danger posed by ambush or trap.
Perception WD/IN/IN Lets you spot, hear, or otherwise detect the presence of something. Measures your general awareness of surroundings and the keenness of your senses. Examples include hearing a conversation through a closed door, eavesdropping under an open window, hearing monsters moving stealthily in the forest, spotting orcs lying in ambush on a road, noticing thugs hiding in shadows, detecting secret doors, or seeing candlelight under a closed door.
