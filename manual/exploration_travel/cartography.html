Cartography System

Overview

The Cartography System is the heart of dungeon exploration. As you venture into labyrinths, ruins, and caverns, you must manually document your discoveries by drawing maps. This transforms exploration from passive wandering into active documentation, creating investment in every floor you explore.

Core Philosophy: Your map is your lifeline. Accurate cartography enables safe navigation around dangers, efficient resource gathering, puzzle solving, and strategic planning. Map completion is rewarded by guild quests and provides crucial tactical advantages.

Grid-Based Movement

All dungeons and the overworld use a grid-based movement system:

  • Dungeon Floors: 35x30 tile grids
  • Overworld: 50x50 tile grid (scalable)
  • Movement: 1 tile = 1 movement step in cardinal directions (N, S, E, W)
  • Turn-Based: Each movement step advances time and may trigger encounters

Drawing Tools

Wall Pencil

Draw or erase walls as you explore. Walls can be single-thick or double-thick depending on dungeon architecture. Mark walls carefully to avoid getting lost or walking into dead ends.

Floor Paint

Color tiles by type to indicate terrain:

Floor Type Color Effect
Standard Gray Normal walkable floor
Water Blue May require swimming or slow movement
Pit/Chasm Black Impassable or causes falling damage
Lava/Fire Red Deals fire damage when crossed
Ice Cyan Slippery, may cause sliding
Poison Green Inflicts poison status

Icon Palette

Place icons to mark important locations. Icons are categorized by function:

Navigation Icons

  • Stairs Up: Ascend to previous floor
  • Stairs Down: Descend to next floor
  • Shortcut (Locked): One-way passage, locked until activated
  • Shortcut (Unlocked): Active shortcut for quick travel
  • Teleporter: Instant transport to another location

Interactive Icons

  • Door (Closed): Unopened door
  • Door (Open): Opened door
  • Locked Door: Requires key, lockpicking, or magic
  • Chest (Unopened): Treasure chest awaiting looting
  • Chest (Opened): Already looted chest
  • Lever/Switch: Activates mechanisms

Gathering Point Icons

  • Mine (Sparkle): Ore gathering point with resources available
  • Mine (Depleted): No resources currently available
  • Chop (Sparkle): Wood gathering point with resources
  • Chop (Depleted): No wood currently available
  • Take (Sparkle): Herb/item gathering point with resources
  • Take (Depleted): No items currently available

Danger Icons

  • FOE (Red): Visible mini-boss enemy, currently pursuing
  • FOE (Yellow): Visible mini-boss enemy, patrolling
  • Trap: Detected trap location
  • Hazard: Environmental danger (poison gas, spikes, etc.)

Miscellaneous Icons

  • NPC: Non-player character location
  • Quest Objective: Location for quest completion
  • Save Point: Safe location to save progress
  • Healing Spring: Restores HP/TP

Text Annotations

Attach custom notes to any tile. Use annotations to record:

  • Puzzle solutions or hints
  • NPC dialogue or requests
  • Environmental hazard warnings
  • FOE movement patterns
  • Resource respawn timers
  • Secret passage locations

Auto-Fill (Optional)

Modern convenience feature that automatically fills explored corridors. Can be toggled on/off. Recommended settings:

  • Dungeons: Manual mapping for full engagement
  • Overworld: Auto-fill after initial exploration to reduce tedium

Dynamic Icon Updates

Many icons change appearance based on status:

Icon Status Change Visual Indicator
Chest Unopened → Opened Lid opens, sparkle disappears
Gathering Point Available → Depleted Sparkle disappears
Gathering Point Depleted → Refreshed Sparkle reappears
Shortcut Locked → Unlocked Color changes to yellow/gold
Door Closed → Open Door graphic opens
FOE Patrolling → Chasing Yellow → Red, pulsing

Strategic Cartography

Planning Safe Routes

Use your map to plot paths that avoid FOE patrol routes. Mark FOE positions and movement patterns with annotations to predict safe windows for passage.

Resource Tracking

Mark all gathering points and note their refresh timers. Efficient resource farming requires knowing which nodes are available and planning optimal collection routes.

Puzzle Documentation

Many dungeons contain spatial puzzles requiring lever sequences, teleporter navigation, or timed movements. Use annotations to record solutions for future reference.

Minimizing Backtracking

Complete maps reveal unexplored branches, preventing wasted time retracing steps. Mark dead ends clearly to avoid repeated exploration.

Guild Mapping Quests

The Adventurer's Guild frequently requests complete floor maps. Rewards scale with map accuracy:

Completion % Reward Tier Typical Rewards
50-69% Bronze Small gold bonus, basic items
70-89% Silver Moderate gold, equipment unlock
90-99% Gold Large gold, rare materials
100% Platinum Skill points, unique equipment, fast travel unlock

Map Persistence

Critical Rule: If your party dies, map progress is retained. Your cartography work is never lost, even in defeat. This allows you to rush back to your previous position on subsequent attempts without re-exploring.

Overworld Cartography

The overworld uses identical mapping tools but with different scale and purpose:

  • Terrain Mapping: Mark forests, mountains, rivers, deserts
  • Dungeon Entrances: Icon all labyrinth locations
  • Town Locations: Mark settlements and their services
  • Overworld FOEs: Document patrol routes of dragons, giants, etc.
  • Landmarks: Note healing springs, campsites, quest locations
  • Fast Travel Points: Mark unlocked waypoints

Cartography Tips

  • Map systematically - complete one section before moving to the next
  • Mark FOE starting positions immediately upon entering a floor
  • Use different annotation colors for different information types
  • Update gathering point icons as soon as they refresh
  • Draw walls as you explore, not from memory later
  • Mark one-way passages clearly to avoid confusion
  • Note environmental hazards before they damage your party
  • Keep quest objective markers updated as you progress
