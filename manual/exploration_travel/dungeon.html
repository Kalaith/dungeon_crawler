Dungeon Exploration

Overview

Dungeons are grid-based labyrinths filled with monsters, treasures, environmental hazards, and powerful FOE enemies. Each dungeon consists of multiple floors (strata) organized thematically, with difficulty increasing as you descend deeper. Exploration requires careful mapping, resource management, and tactical awareness.

Dungeon Structure

Strata System

Dungeons are divided into thematic sections called Strata, each containing 3-5 floors:

Stratum Floors Theme New Mechanics
First 1-5 Introduction Basic enemies, simple layouts
Second 6-10 Escalation Environmental hazards, FOE puzzles
Third 11-15 Challenge Complex FOE patterns, gathering emphasis
Fourth 16-20 Mastery Multi-floor puzzles, boss encounters

Boss Gates: Each stratum ends with a boss encounter that must be defeated to progress to the next stratum. Bosses are significantly more powerful than regular enemies and require strategic party composition and preparation.

Floor Layouts

  • Grid Size: 35x30 tiles per floor
  • Complexity: Increases with depth - early floors are linear, later floors have branching paths and loops
  • Shortcuts: One-way passages that unlock after activation, allowing quick return to town
  • Save Points: Safe tiles where you can save progress without returning to town

FOE System (Formido Oppugnatura Exsequens)

What Are FOEs?

FOEs are visible mini-boss enemies that roam dungeon floors according to specific movement patterns. They appear as colored orbs on your map and represent significantly higher threat levels than random encounters.

Warning: FOEs can often one-shot unprepared parties. Avoid combat until you're confident in your party's strength, or use them as skill gates to test your progression.

FOE Characteristics

Trait Description
Visible Always shown on map, unlike random encounters
Mobile Move according to specific AI patterns
Avoidable Combat only triggers on collision
Powerful Much higher stats than regular enemies
Respawning Respawn after leaving floor or resting

FOE Movement Patterns

Understanding FOE movement is crucial for safe navigation:

Pattern Behavior Strategy
Static Never moves Easy to avoid, guards specific areas
Patrol Fixed route, repeating Time your movements between patrols
Mirror Moves same direction as player Move perpendicular to create distance
Inverse Moves opposite of player Use to manipulate position
Chase Pursues when detected (3-5 tile range) Stay out of detection radius
Random Moves unpredictably Difficult to plan around, high risk
Phasing Can move through walls Cannot be blocked, requires speed
Fast Moves 2 tiles per player turn Requires careful planning, very dangerous

FOE Detection

The UI provides FOE proximity warnings:

  • Yellow Icon: FOE is patrolling normally
  • Red Icon (Pulsing): FOE has detected you and is pursuing
  • Audio Cue: Distinct sound when FOE enters detection range
  • Line of Sight: FOEs cannot detect through walls

FOE Combat

If you collide with a FOE, combat initiates immediately:

  • FOEs have 3-5x the HP of regular enemies
  • FOEs deal significantly more damage
  • Escaping is harder due to FOE's high AGI stats
  • Defeating FOEs yields superior materials and experience
  • Some FOEs drop unique equipment or unlock shortcuts

Random Encounters

Encounter Rate

Random encounters occur as you move through the dungeon:

  • Frequency: Approximately every 5-10 steps
  • Variability: Affected by party's Luck stat and certain skills
  • Avoidance: Some skills can reduce encounter rate
  • Danger Level: Every battle is meaningful - regular enemies can kill unprepared parties

Enemy Difficulty

Unlike typical RPGs, regular encounters demand respect:

  • Enemies can deal 20-40% of a character's HP per hit
  • Status effects (poison, paralysis, sleep) are genuinely dangerous
  • Enemy groups can focus-fire vulnerable party members
  • Buffs and debuffs significantly affect combat outcomes

Resource Gathering

Gathering Points

Specific tiles contain resource nodes that yield crafting materials:

Type Icon Materials Required Skill
Mine Pickaxe Ores, gems, minerals Mining
Chop Axe Wood, bark, sap Lumberjacking
Take Hand Herbs, flowers, insects Herbalism

Gathering Mechanics

  • Sparkle Effect: Gathering points sparkle when resources are available
  • Refresh Timer: Points refresh after time passes (varies by node)
  • Skill Dependency: Higher gathering skills yield better/more materials
  • Randomized Loot: Each gathering attempt draws from a loot table
  • Rare Materials: Low chance for exceptional materials at any node

Dungeon Options

Map

Access your cartography screen to view and edit your floor map. See the Cartography System for full details on mapping mechanics.

Open Door

Interact with doors to proceed through the dungeon. Doors may be:

  • Unlocked: Opens freely
  • Locked: Requires key, lockpicking, or magic
  • One-Way: Can only be opened from one side
  • Shortcut: Unlocks permanently after first activation

Locked Door Options

Method Requirements Result
Pick Lock Locks skill, lockpicks Quiet, preserves door
Break Down High Strength Loud, destroys door permanently
Foramen Spell Magic user, spell known Unlocks permanently, silent
Use Key Correct key in inventory Instant, quiet

Close Door

Close doors behind you to prevent enemies from following. Broken doors cannot be closed.

Open Chest

Treasure chests contain equipment, consumables, and gold. Front character opens the chest and triggers any traps:

  • Trap Types: Poison gas, explosion, curse, mimic (chest is actually a monster)
  • Detection: High Perception may detect traps before opening
  • Disarming: Rogues with Traps skill can attempt to disarm
  • Risk/Reward: Better chests often have deadlier traps

Gather Resources

Interact with sparkling gathering points to collect materials. Success rate and yield depend on relevant gathering skill level.

Save Progress

At designated save points, you can save your game without returning to town. Save points are marked on your map.

Dungeon Strategy

Party Formation

Your party is arranged in two rows, which determines their role in combat and exploration.

Hazard Effect Mitigation
Poison Gas Inflicts poison status Antidotes, poison resistance
Spike Traps Deals physical damage High Perception to detect
Lava Tiles Fire damage per step Fire resistance equipment
Ice Floors Sliding, loss of control Careful movement, ice cleats
Darkness Reduced vision, ambushes Light sources, Fiat Lux spell
Cursed Zones Stat debuffs, HP drain Clerical magic, holy items
Row Best Character Type Role
Front Warriors, Paladins, Battle-Mages Engage enemies in melee, protect the back row. High AC/HP recommended.
Back Archers, Healers, Squishy Casters Attack from safety, cast support spells. Protected from most melee attacks.

Note: There are no class restrictions on rows. A Wizard with high AC and touch spells can effectively fight in the Front Row.

Resource Management

  • HP/TP Conservation: Don't waste resources on trivial encounters
  • Healing Items: Keep readily accessible in left hand slots
  • Inventory Space: Leave room for gathered materials and loot
  • Return Timing: Head back to town before resources are depleted

FOE Navigation

  • Map FOE starting positions immediately upon entering a floor
  • Observe movement patterns for at least 3-4 turns before proceeding
  • Use annotations to mark safe routes and danger zones
  • Plan escape routes in case FOE detection occurs
  • Some FOE puzzles require manipulating FOE positions with careful movement

Exploration Tips

  • Map systematically - complete one section before moving to the next
  • Mark all gathering points and note refresh timers
  • Save frequently at save points
  • Keep healing items ready for trap triggers
  • Listen at doors before opening (may hear enemies)
  • Check party HP/TP regularly
  • Don't overextend - return to town before party is weakened
  • Complete mapping quests for guild rewards
