Eating & Drinking

Overview

After a hard day's travel, your characters are hungry, their throats are parched, and they're all looking forward to a good meal. Managing your party's hunger and thirst is essential for survival.

Food: Rations

Characters eat "rations," picnic style meals with good helpings from each food group. Nearly every chandler's shop offers rations, and your party may occasionally find several left behind by wandering groups or hunt down dinner in the wild.

About Rations:

  • Complete meals in portable form
  • Available at most chandler's shops
  • Can be found during exploration
  • Can be obtained through hunting
  • Essential for maintaining hunger levels

Water

Along with rations, characters take water to soothe their palates. Carrying their waterskins everywhere, characters may refill their skins at the many streams and rivers that weave through the game world.

About Water:

  • Carried in waterskins
  • Can be refilled at streams and rivers
  • Free and readily available from natural sources
  • Essential for maintaining thirst levels

How To Feed And Give Drink

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Review Character: Open the character review screen
  2. Select Item: Pick up an appropriate item (a waterskin or ration) from the inventory by clicking on it with the left mouse button
  3. Use Mouth Icon: Click on the mouth icon
  4. Satisfaction: The character's hunger or thirst lines, depending on what you fed them, will reflect the satisfaction of these basic needs

Note: This icon (mouth) is also used should you wish to consume a herb or potion.

Hunger and Thirst Indicators

Characters have hunger and thirst lines that show their current satisfaction levels. Monitor these regularly to ensure your party stays healthy and effective.

Need Item Required Source
Hunger Rations Chandler's shops, found items, hunting
Thirst Water (in waterskins) Streams, rivers, wells

Special Rules

No Weight Gain:

Unlike Earth, characters can eat all they like without gaining weight.

Invalid Food Items:

If you try to feed a character something that's not normally eaten (like leather boots), the system will let you know and prevent the action.

Consumption Strategy

When To Eat and Drink:

  • Before Travel: Ensure characters are well-fed before long journeys
  • During Camping: Rest periods are ideal times to eat and drink
  • After Combat: Replenish after strenuous activity
  • Before Status Drops: Don't wait until hunger/thirst becomes critical

Resource Management:

  • Always carry extra rations and waterskins
  • Refill waterskins whenever you encounter water sources
  • Purchase rations when visiting towns
  • Consider hunting to supplement food supplies
  • Monitor party supplies to avoid running out in the wilderness

Effects Of Hunger and Thirst

Low Hunger Effects:

  • Reduced effectiveness in combat
  • Lower movement points
  • Decreased attribute performance
  • Potential health deterioration over time

Low Thirst Effects:

  • Reduced endurance
  • Lower skill performance
  • Decreased overall effectiveness
  • Potential health issues if prolonged

Potions and Herbs

The mouth icon is also used for consuming potions and herbs. These items provide various benefits beyond simple nourishment:

  • Healing Potions: Restore life points
  • Astral Potions: Restore astral points for magic users
  • Herbs: Various effects depending on type
  • Antidotes: Cure poisoning
  • Remedies: Treat diseases

Quick Access: For faster consumption during combat, keep important potions or herbs in your left hand slot for immediate use.
